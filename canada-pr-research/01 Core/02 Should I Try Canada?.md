# Should I Try Canada?

## Purpose
This note is for rational evaluation of whether pursuing Canada is a good idea under current conditions.

## Decision Question
- Main question: Should I spend serious time, money, and energy trying to build a future in Canada?

## Reasons To Go
- Hong Kong development prospects currently appear weak
- Existing OWP creates a real opportunity window
- Canada may provide broader long-term career and residency options
- A structured trial could create better information than staying uncertain indefinitely
- To Research:
  - Which realistic job paths could support survival
  - Whether specific provinces are a better fit than major cities

## Reasons Not To Go
- No strong Canada support network
- English communication weakness may make job search and adaptation harder
- Mental health risk may increase under isolation, financial stress, and uncertainty
- Relocation costs may be high relative to the amount of reliable information currently available
- PR is not guaranteed even with legal work status

## Main Risks
- Burning savings without job traction
- Arriving without a stable plan and entering survival stress
- Depression worsening during job search or early settlement
- Choosing a province based on immigration talk rather than actual job reality
- Wasting OWP time through delay and indecision

## Possible Upside
- Better long-term career trajectory than Hong Kong
- New job market and immigration options
- Potential route toward PR if a realistic job and province match is found
- Better medium-term flexibility if the first year is survivable

## Personal Cost
- Money spent on relocation and settlement
- Loss of routine and current stability
- High uncertainty during job search
- Social isolation risk
- Need to operate in English more often

## Mental Health Concerns
- Migration stress can amplify depression
- Isolation and low confidence can reduce follow-through
- A hard landing may damage both finances and mental stability
- Any move should include protective measures, stop conditions, and reduced-chaos planning

See [[Canada-PR-Research/05 Planning/03 Mental Health and Sustainability]].

## Financial Runway
- Current base estimate: around HKD 600,000
- Unknowns:
  - CAD conversion rate to use for planning
  - Canada settlement burn rate by province
  - Minimum emergency reserve that must not be touched

See [[Canada-PR-Research/05 Planning/02 Financial Planning]].

## Conditions Under Which I Should Go
- OWP timeline is still usable
- Chosen province/city has a realistic first-year survival path
- Budget cap and stop-loss rules are defined
- There is a shortlist of target job types with plausible demand
- Mental health support plan is prepared

## Conditions Under Which I Should Delay
- OWP timeline is unclear
- Budget assumptions are still vague
- No province stands out as even moderately realistic
- English/job readiness gap needs focused work first
- Depression is currently too destabilizing for a major transition

## Conditions Under Which I Should Stop
- OWP window no longer supports a rational move
- Cost of trial becomes too high relative to likely upside
- No realistic job path emerges after structured research
- Mental health risk becomes unacceptably high
- The plan depends mainly on hope, luck, or unverified assumptions

## Evidence To Gather Before Deciding
- [ ] Exact OWP timeline and use strategy
- [ ] 2-3 province options with concrete tradeoffs
- [ ] 2-3 realistic job paths with job-market evidence
- [ ] First-year budget estimate
- [ ] Clear stop-loss thresholds
