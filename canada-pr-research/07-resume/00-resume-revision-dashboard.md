# Resume Revision Dashboard

## Purpose
This area is for preparing a Canada-targeted resume workflow: storing the current baseline version, investigating market expectations, and tracking recommended edits before sending applications.

## Main Goal
- Build one strong Canada-ready base resume that can later be tailored for specific roles such as IT Support, QA Tester, Application Support, Operations Support, or Junior Developer.

## Workflow
1. Put the untouched current resume in [[canada-pr-research/07-resume/01-resume-source/readme]].
2. Review [[canada-pr-research/07-resume/02-cv-investigation/canada-resume-expectations]] for formatting and content rules.
3. Map your real experience against target roles in [[canada-pr-research/07-resume/02-cv-investigation/target-role-resume-strategy]].
4. Apply the rewrite ideas in [[canada-pr-research/07-resume/03-revision-notes/proposed-resume-improvements]].
5. Track unresolved gaps and questions in [[canada-pr-research/07-resume/03-revision-notes/resume-revision-backlog]].

## Current Assumptions
- You need a practical Canada job-search resume, not an academic CV.
- The first target should be a broad, adaptable base resume rather than many separate versions at once.
- Communication strength on paper should be improved by clearer writing, tighter bullets, and stronger evidence of outcomes.
- Resume direction should stay aligned with the job paths already tracked in [[Canada-PR-Research/04 Job Paths/01 Job Paths Overview]].

## Scope Guardrails
- Do not invent achievements
- Do not over-specialize the first draft before choosing a target role
- Do not leave vague bullets like "responsible for" if stronger evidence is available
- Do not optimize for Hong Kong-style resume habits if they reduce Canada job-market fit
