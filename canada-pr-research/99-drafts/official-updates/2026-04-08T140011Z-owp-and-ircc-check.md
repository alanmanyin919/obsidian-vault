## What Changed
- Material OWP change since older baseline notes: IRCC updated the spouse/family OWP eligibility page on 2026-03-23 to add a British Columbia significant investment project (SIP) exception. Under that exception, a spouse can apply for an open work permit if the principal worker has a SIP work permit or letter of introduction, is employed or has a job offer at any TEER level, and will live in Canada while employed. IRCC also says the usual 16-month remaining-validity requirement does not apply for this SIP case.
- Material PR-planning change still in force: IRCC announced the 2026 Express Entry category set on 2026-02-18. New categories are medical doctors with Canadian work experience, researchers with Canadian work experience, senior managers with Canadian work experience, transport occupations, and skilled military recruits with a Canadian Armed Forces job offer. Renewed categories are French-language proficiency, health care and social services, education, STEM, and trades. IRCC also raised the minimum work experience for all renewed categories from 6 months to 1 year in the previous 3 years.
- Draw pattern check: as of this run, the latest official Express Entry round in IRCC’s feed is 2026-04-02, a trades category draw with 3,000 invitations and a CRS cutoff of 477. The most recent CEC draw listed is 2026-03-31 with 2,250 invitations and a CRS cutoff of 509. No newer round was visible on the official IRCC feed during this check.

## What Did Not Change
- The 2025-01-21 family OWP tightening remains in place for most workers: only select spouses qualify, dependent children are no longer eligible under this measure, and the general rule still says the principal worker normally needs at least 16 months left on their work permit when the spouse applies.
- IRCC still states that previously approved family OWPs remain valid, and in-Canada family members can still apply to extend shorter permits to match the principal applicant if they apply before expiry and meet the extension conditions.
- IRCC still lists PR-linked streams and pilots that can support spouse OWP eligibility, including CEC, FSW, FST, PNP, AIP, FCIP, RCIP, Agri-Food, and some Quebec-linked cases.
- No new April 2026 IRCC notice was found that changes BOWP rules or general OWP validity rules for PR applicants beyond the items above.

## Relevance To My Situation
- If planning depends on a spouse or dependent-child OWP, the January 21, 2025 restrictions are still the default rule. That keeps timing and eligibility tighter unless the case fits a listed PR-linked pathway or the new BC SIP exception.
- For PR planning, the 2026 Express Entry setup still favors people who can qualify through CEC, French proficiency, or one of the renewed or new occupational categories. The increase from 6 months to 1 year of experience for renewed categories raises the threshold and may delay eligibility for some category-based strategies.
- The latest official draw data still shows active CEC and category-based rounds, so PR timing for in-Canada workers remains connected to CRS competitiveness and category fit rather than to any new broad OWP relief.

## Uncertainties
- I did not find a new IRCC notice after 2026-03-27 specifically changing general OWP or BOWP policy, but some operational pages can update without a separate news notice.
- The new BC SIP spouse-OWP exception is narrow and only matters if the work authorization is under a qualifying provincial SIP arrangement in British Columbia.
- Personal impact depends on whether the actual plan is single-applicant, includes a spouse/dependants, and aims for CEC, PNP, or another PR-linked route.
