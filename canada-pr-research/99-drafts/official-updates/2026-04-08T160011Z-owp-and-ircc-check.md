## What Changed
- On 2026-03-23, IRCC updated the family OWP eligibility page to add a narrow new exception for British Columbia significant investment project workers: their spouse can apply for an open work permit even if the principal worker is in any TEER level, and the usual 16-month remaining-validity requirement does not apply for that spouse measure.
- On 2026-03-13, IRCC announced a new Quebec temporary measure for certain workers already on employer-specific permits and invited by Quebec to submit a permanent selection application. Eligible workers can get an employer-specific permit for up to 12 more months, with applications accepted until 2026-12-31.
- On 2026-02-18, IRCC announced the 2026 Express Entry category setup. New categories were added for medical doctors with Canadian work experience, researchers with Canadian work experience, senior managers with Canadian work experience, transport occupations, and skilled military recruits with a Canadian Armed Forces job offer.
- Also on 2026-02-18, IRCC said the renewed 2026 categories are French-language proficiency, health care and social services, education, STEM, and trades, and increased the minimum work experience for all renewed categories from 6 months to 1 year within the previous 3 years.
- IRCC also said Canadian Experience Class draws continued through early 2026, which matters for in-Canada applicants planning around current work authorization.

## What Did Not Change
- The special open work permit for pending 2021 TR-to-PR pathway applicants is still available until 2026-12-31. If the principal applicant already has a valid work permit, they can still apply up to 4 months before expiry.
- The broad family OWP tightening from 2025 is still in place. For most foreign workers, only spouses of certain workers remain eligible for new family OWPs; dependent children are generally no longer eligible under that family OWP measure.
- Low-skilled workers who are not on a PR pathway still do not have new family OWP access; IRCC still only shows limited in-Canada extension options where a family member already holds a shorter existing permit.
- The visitor-to-work-permit in-Canada temporary policy remains ended as of 2024-08-28.
- I did not find a new 2026-04-08 IRCC announcement changing OWP validity rules, entry-window rules, or broad PR eligibility rules beyond the February and March 2026 updates above.

## Relevance To My Situation
- The most important current planning change is on the PR side, not general OWP access: Express Entry in 2026 is more targeted, and the renewed categories now require 1 year of eligible experience instead of 6 months. That can delay eligibility if planning relied on a shorter work-history window.
- Canadian Experience Class draws continuing into early 2026 is still positive for someone already working in Canada, but the advantage is now more dependent on actual Canadian work experience and category fit.
- If my situation involves a standard OWP and a spouse or dependent, the 2025 family OWP restrictions are still the main rule set. The new BC significant investment project exception is narrow and likely irrelevant unless tied to that specific pathway.
- The Quebec measure may matter only if I am in Quebec on an employer-specific permit and already invited by Quebec into its permanent selection process.
- The TR-to-PR 2021 OWP extension remains useful only for people in that older backlog; it is not a general new OWP route.
