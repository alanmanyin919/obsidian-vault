## What Changed
- IRCC extended the TR to PR pathway open work permit application deadline to December 31, 2026 for eligible 2021 TR to PR applicants and eligible family members. The page was modified on 2026-03-12.
- IRCC announced a new Quebec-focused temporary measure on 2026-03-13. Eligible workers invited by Quebec to submit a DSP under the new Skilled Worker Selection Program can get an employer-specific work permit for up to 12 more months, with applications open until December 31, 2026.
- Express Entry planning changed at the policy level in 2026: IRCC confirmed new 2026 categories including medical doctors with Canadian work experience and in-Canada skilled workers in strategic sectors, while renewed categories include French-language proficiency, health care and social services, education, STEM, and trades. IRCC also raised the minimum work experience for renewed categories from 6 months to 1 year.
- The latest official Express Entry rounds visible in IRCC’s live JSON at check time were:
- 2026-04-02: Trades Occupations, 3,000 invitations, CRS 477.
- 2026-03-31: Canadian Experience Class, 2,250 invitations, CRS 509.
- 2026-03-30: Provincial Nominee Program, 356 invitations, CRS 802.

## What Did Not Change
- I did not find a newer IRCC OWP rule change after the 2026-03-12 TR to PR OWP extension and the 2026-03-13 Quebec worker measure.
- The family OWP tightening that took effect on January 21, 2025 still appears to remain in place: most eligibility is still limited to spouses of certain workers, dependent children are generally no longer newly eligible under that measure, and the 16-month remaining-validity rule still applies for many worker-based spouse cases.
- The core TR to PR pathway remains closed for new PR applications; the current operational change is continued OWP access for eligible people already in the 2021 backlog.
- I did not find a newer Express Entry category announcement after IRCC’s 2026 category update published on 2026-02-18.

## Relevance To My Situation
- If my OWP planning depends on the old 2021 TR to PR pathway, the practical deadline is now December 31, 2026, not an earlier date. That reduces immediate expiry pressure for people who are actually eligible under that specific public policy.
- If I am not in that 2021 TR to PR cohort, this extension does not create a new OWP option for me.
- The Quebec measure is only relevant if I am working in Quebec, hold or recently held an employer-specific permit there, and am being transitioned through Quebec’s skilled worker selection process. Otherwise it is not a general Canada-wide OWP route.
- For PR strategy, the latest Express Entry pattern still supports in-Canada skilled work as a useful route: CEC draws are active, and category-based draws continue for trades and other priority sectors. That means Canadian skilled work experience remains important for timing and competitiveness.
- The increased 1-year experience threshold for renewed categories matters for planning. If I am relying on category-based selection, 6 months of eligible work is no longer enough for those renewed categories.
