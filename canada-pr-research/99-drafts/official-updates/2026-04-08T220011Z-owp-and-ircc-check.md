## What Changed
- No new major IRCC OWP or PR-pathway rule change was found on official IRCC sources between the latest published items and this check on 2026-04-08T220011Z.
- The most relevant still-current OWP rule change remains the IRCC update for spouses of workers on significant investment projects in British Columbia. As of 2026-03-23, that route no longer requires the worker's status to remain valid for 16 months after the spouse's open work permit application is received.
- The 2026 Express Entry category reset remains in force from IRCC's 2026-02-18 update. New categories include physicians, researchers, senior managers, transport, and skilled military recruits with Canadian work experience or qualifying job offers where applicable. Renewed categories include French, healthcare and social services, education, STEM, and trades. IRCC also raised the minimum work experience for renewed categories from 6 months to 1 year in the previous 3 years.

- A Quebec-specific measure published 2026-03-13 remains active: some eligible Quebec temporary foreign workers invited to submit a DSP can get an employer-specific work permit for up to 12 more months, with permits expiring up to 2026-12-31 in scope. This is not a general Canada-wide OWP change.

## What Did Not Change
- The broad tightening of open work permits for family members of temporary residents announced in January 2025 is still in force.
- For high-skilled workers not on an eligible PR pathway, the standard spouse/common-law-partner OWP route still generally requires the worker's permit or authorized work to remain valid for at least 16 months after IRCC receives the spouse's application.
- IRCC still lists Canadian Experience Class, Federal Skilled Worker, Federal Skilled Trades, and Provincial Nominee-linked pathways as core PR planning routes relevant to workers already in Canada.
- No new April 2026 IRCC notice or backgrounder was found that changed general OWP validity, general entry-window rules, or general usage rules for OWP holders.
