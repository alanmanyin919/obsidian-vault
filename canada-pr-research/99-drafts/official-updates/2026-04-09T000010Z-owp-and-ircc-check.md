## What Changed
- No new April 9, 2026 IRCC rule change was found on the official pages checked for OWP usage, validity, or PR-pathway access.
- Recent official changes still relevant:
- IRCC announced the 2026 Express Entry category set on 2026-02-27. The active categories remain French-language proficiency, health care and social services, trades, education, and agriculture/agri-food. STEM is not listed as a 2026 category.
- IRCC states candidates need at least 1 year of full-time continuous work experience in the last 3 years in an eligible occupation to qualify for a category-based round.
- On 2026-03-13, IRCC introduced a Quebec-specific temporary measure for certain workers whose Quebec pathway is in progress. This is an employer-specific permit measure, not a general OWP expansion.

## What Did Not Change
- The family OWP restriction announced for 2025 remains in effect on the official IRCC notice checked: spouses of foreign workers are limited to specified TEER and sector cases, and the principal worker must have at least 16 months left on their permit when the spouse applies.
- IRCC’s bridging open work permit route still exists for eligible PR applicants already in Canada and is still separate from Quebec cases.
- IRCC’s public policy page for certain permanent residence applicants still shows an open work permit route that can be used by some in-Canada PR applicants, including timing conditions tied to current status and work permit expiry.
- Express Entry still relies on regular rounds plus category-based rounds. No official page checked showed a new cancellation or suspension of CEC/category-based selection as of this check.

## Relevance To My Situation
- This check does not show a new emergency change today, but the 2026 category-based selection setup matters for planning. If your work history was being aimed at a STEM category, that route looks weaker because STEM is not part of the announced 2026 category set.
- If you expect to rely on a spouse/family OWP route, the 2025 restrictions still create timing and eligibility pressure.
- If your plan is to stay in Canada on work authorization while moving toward PR, the main practical tracks still appear to be CEC or another eligible PR stream followed by the appropriate in-Canada work permit option, rather than any new broad OWP concession.
