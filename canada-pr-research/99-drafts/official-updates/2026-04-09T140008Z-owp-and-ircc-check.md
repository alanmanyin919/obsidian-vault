## What Changed
- No new broad OWP rule change was found on April 9, 2026 from the IRCC sources checked.
- The most relevant recent federal PR-planning change still in force is IRCC's February 18, 2026 Express Entry update:
  - 2026 new categories: medical doctors with Canadian work experience, researchers with Canadian work experience, senior managers with Canadian work experience, transport occupations, and skilled military recruits with a CAF job offer.
  - Renewed categories remain French-language proficiency, health care and social services, education, STEM, and trades.
  - For renewed categories, the minimum eligible work experience was raised from 6 months to 1 year.
- The latest official Express Entry rounds visible as of this check continue to include CEC and category-based draws:
  - April 2, 2026: Trades, 3,000 invitations, CRS 477
  - March 31, 2026: Canadian Experience Class, 2,250 invitations, CRS 509
  - March 30, 2026: Provincial Nominee Program, 356 invitations, CRS 802
  - March 18, 2026: French-language proficiency, 4,000 invitations, CRS 393
  - March 17, 2026: Canadian Experience Class, 4,000 invitations, CRS 507
  - March 16, 2026: Provincial Nominee Program, 362 invitations, CRS 742
- A separate March 13, 2026 IRCC measure created a Quebec-only employer-specific work permit option for certain workers on a PR path. It is not a general OWP change, but it may matter if Quebec becomes relevant.

## What Did Not Change
- IRCC's BOWP page still shows the core rule set for PR applicants already in Canada rather than a new expansion or cutoff:
  - most BOWP pathways still require the person to live in Canada when applying
  - for most non-Quebec classes, the page still says the applicant must intend to live outside Quebec
  - the page still recognizes valid status, maintained status, or eligibility to restore status in the listed scenarios
- The January 21, 2025 family-OWP tightening still appears to be the operative baseline:
  - spouses of foreign workers are restricted to specified occupation groups and permit-length conditions
  - spouses of workers transitioning to permanent residence are still stated to be not impacted by those 2025 restrictions
- No new IRCC notice was found in 2026 announcing a broad new OWP validity extension, entry-window change, or general usage-rule rewrite for OWP holders.
- No new IRCC announcement was found in this check showing a pause or shutdown of CEC draws. CEC draws are still happening, but at relatively high CRS cutoffs.

## Relevance To My Situation
- This is mostly a no-change check for OWP rules. There is no new official sign in this run that OWP holders suddenly gained extra time or a new federal PR bridge.
- The practical planning signal is in Express Entry draw behavior, not in a new OWP rule:
  - CEC draws are active, which is positive if Canadian skilled work experience is realistic.
  - Recent CEC cutoffs at 507 to 509 are still high, so timing and score competitiveness remain important.
  - French draws remain materially lower than recent CEC cutoffs, which may matter if French becomes a realistic score lever.
- If the plan depends on a spouse or partner OWP, the tighter 2025 family-OWP rules still need to be treated as current.
- The March 13 Quebec measure does not change general Canada-wide OWP planning, but it is a reminder that some PR-transition support is now being done through targeted employer-specific permits rather than broad OWPs.
