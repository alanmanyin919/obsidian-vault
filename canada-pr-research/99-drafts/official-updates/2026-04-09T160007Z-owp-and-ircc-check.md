## What Changed
- No same-day major IRCC policy change was found on 2026-04-09, but recent official changes still relevant to planning are active.
- The general IRCC open work permit page was updated on 2026-03-31. It still confirms that OWPs remain limited to specific situations, not a general work authorization route.
- The family-member OWP rules remain tighter than before. Since 2025-01-21, only select spouses qualify, dependent children generally do not get new family OWPs under this measure, and low-skilled-worker family access remains shut for new applications. The eligibility page also shows a new 2026-03-23 rule for spouses of workers under a SIP agreement.
- The TR-to-PR pathway OWP measure was extended again: eligible 2021 TR-to-PR applicants can apply until 2026-12-31. This is a real extension, but it is narrow and only helps that older cohort.
- A new Quebec-specific transition measure was announced on 2026-03-13. It gives certain workers with employer-specific permits expiring by 2026-12-31 a path to another employer-specific permit while Quebec PR processing continues. This is not a general Canada-wide OWP improvement, but it does show IRCC is using targeted bridge measures instead of broad reopening.
- For PR planning, the biggest recent federal change is Express Entry category design for 2026. New categories were announced for physicians with Canadian work experience, researchers with Canadian work experience, senior managers with Canadian work experience, transport occupations, and skilled military recruits. Renewed categories remain French, health and social services, education, STEM, and trades.
- IRCC also raised the minimum work experience for the renewed 2026 categories from 6 months to 12 months in an eligible occupation over the previous 3 years. That is a meaningful eligibility tightening for category-based draws.

## What Did Not Change
- No official source checked today showed a broad new OWP validity extension or broader nationwide reopening for general OWP holders.
- No official source checked today showed a change to the standard BOWP core logic: PR applicants still generally need a complete PR application, acknowledgement of receipt, and valid status or maintained/restorable worker status to bridge.
- No official source checked today showed a new Canada-wide PR pathway created specifically for OWP holders as a group.
- Canadian Experience Class remains part of Express Entry, and category-based selection still supplements rather than replaces general or program-specific rounds.
- The 2024 rule ending the in-Canada visitor-to-work-permit temporary public policy still stands; no reversal was found.

## Relevance To My Situation
