## What Changed
- IRCC's family-OWP rules now include a new carve-out effective 2026-03-23 for spouses of workers on significant investment projects in British Columbia. On the official eligibility page, those spouses can apply even if the principal worker is at any TEER level, and the normal 16-month validity requirement does not apply for that SIP measure.
- IRCC confirmed the 2026 Express Entry category set on 2026-02-18. New categories are medical doctors with Canadian work experience, researchers with Canadian work experience, senior managers with Canadian work experience, transport occupations, and skilled military recruits with a Canadian Armed Forces job offer.
- IRCC also tightened the renewed category threshold for 2026. For French-language, health and social services, education, STEM, and trades categories, the minimum eligible work experience was raised from 6 months to 12 months within the previous 3 years.
- IRCC said Canadian Experience Class draws continued through early 2026, alongside French-proficiency draws.
- On 2026-03-13, IRCC announced a Quebec-specific temporary measure allowing certain workers invited to submit a Quebec DSP to get an employer-specific work permit for up to 12 more months while Quebec reviews CSQ-stage eligibility. This is a PR-transition measure, but it is not a general open work permit measure.
