## What Changed
- No major new same-day IRCC change was found on 2026-04-09 for general OWP rules or PR-pathway access.
- The most important recent official change still affecting planning is the 2026 Express Entry category update announced on 2026-02-18. IRCC added new categories for medical doctors with Canadian work experience, researchers with Canadian work experience, senior managers with Canadian work experience, transport occupations, and certain skilled military recruits with a Canadian Armed Forces job offer.
- IRCC also kept French-language proficiency, health care and social services, education, STEM, and trades as renewed categories, but raised the minimum work-experience requirement for all renewed categories from 6 months to 1 year in an eligible occupation over the previous 3 years.
- The latest official Express Entry draw data visible at check time shows targeted draws continuing into early April 2026: 2026-04-02 trades category draw, 2026-03-31 Canadian Experience Class draw, and 2026-03-30 Provincial Nominee Program draw.
- A Quebec-specific transition measure announced on 2026-03-13 lets some eligible workers on a Quebec PR track apply for an employer-specific work permit for up to 12 more months, with applications accepted until 2026-12-31.

## What Did Not Change
- The family-member OWP restrictions that took effect on 2025-01-21 still appear unchanged in the checked IRCC notice.
- Under that notice, spouses of foreign workers still generally need the principal worker to have at least 16 months remaining on their work permit when the spouse applies, and dependent children of foreign workers still remain ineligible for family OWPs under that policy.
- IRCC still says already-approved family OWPs remain valid until expiry, and some in-Canada renewals remain possible if they match the principal applicant's underlying status.
- The checked official sources did not show a new broad IRCC update changing general OWP validity, entry-window rules, or a new nationwide OWP relief measure for typical non-exempt holders.
- The checked official sources also did not show a new all-program Express Entry draw pattern; recent draws remain targeted around CEC, PNP, and selected categories.

## Relevance To My Situation
- If planning depends on category-based Express Entry eligibility, the biggest urgency point is the 2026 shift from 6 months to 1 year of qualifying experience for renewed categories. Any plan that assumed category eligibility after only 6 months is weaker now.
- If the goal is PR through in-Canada work, continued CEC draws are still a positive signal, but they help mainly after gaining qualifying Canadian skilled work experience and a competitive CRS score.
- If the situation involves a spouse or dependent trying to use a family OWP route, the 2025 restriction set still matters and no easing was found in this check.
- The Quebec-specific 12-month employer-specific permit measure matters only if the pathway is tied to Quebec's permanent selection process. It is not a general Canada-wide OWP change.

## Uncertainties
- This run checked federal IRCC sources only, as instructed. It did not separately review province-specific PNP pages for this task.
- The Express Entry rounds page is partly JavaScript-driven, so the official IRCC JSON feed was used to confirm the most recent draw details.
- No separate new IRCC page specific to bridging open work permits was identified during this run, so this note should not be read as a fresh BOWP-specific confirmation.
- The Quebec measure is clearly official but applies only to a narrow Quebec cohort, so it may be irrelevant unless that exact situation applies.
