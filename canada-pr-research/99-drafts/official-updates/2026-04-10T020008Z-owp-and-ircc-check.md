## What Changed
- No major nationwide OWP rule change was found on IRCC pages between the last few days of March 2026 and this check on 2026-04-10.
- A recent official change remains relevant: as of 2026-03-23, spouses of workers on significant investment projects in British Columbia can apply for an open work permit at any TEER level, and the usual 16-month remaining-validity test does not apply for that SIP case.
- A recent PR-transition measure remains relevant: on 2026-03-13, IRCC announced a temporary Quebec measure allowing certain workers with employer-specific permits expiring by 2026-12-31 and already invited by Quebec to continue toward permanent residence through an employer-specific work permit under the International Mobility Program.
- The current 2026 Express Entry category framework remains the key planning change this year: on 2026-02-18, IRCC confirmed new categories for researchers, senior managers, transport occupations, and skilled military recruits, while continuing French-language, health and social services, education, STEM, and trades categories.
- The category-based selection page now shows the 2026 category list directly on the official page, including physicians, researchers, and senior managers with Canadian work experience.

## What Did Not Change
- I did not find a new broad IRCC announcement changing general OWP validity, entry-window, or basic usage rules for most applicants since the March 2026 updates above.
- The January 21, 2025 family-member OWP tightening still appears to remain in force: spouses of foreign workers are generally limited to TEER 0 or 1, or selected TEER 2 or 3 occupations, unless they fall into a listed exception.
- The temporary policy that let visitors in Canada apply for a work permit from inside Canada still appears to remain ended, effective 2024-08-28.
- The TR to PR pathway is still closed to new PR applicants, but eligible 2021 applicants waiting for a decision can still apply for an open work permit until 2026-12-31.
- Express Entry still does not award CRS points for job offers, a rule that has remained in force since 2025-03-25.

## Relevance To My Situation
- If your planning depends on a normal OWP or a spouse OWP outside special programs, this check does not show a new broad easing. That means timing and eligibility should still be planned conservatively under the tighter post-2025 rules.
- The 2026 Express Entry category list matters more than a general OWP change right now. If your profile can fit Canadian Experience Class, French-language strength, or one of the active occupational categories, that may affect urgency and job-targeting more than waiting for a general OWP policy shift.

- The Quebec 2026 measure is important only if your case is Quebec-linked and already on a province-supported PR track. Otherwise it does not materially improve a general Canada-wide OWP strategy.
