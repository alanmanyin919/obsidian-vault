## What Changed
- A new Worker Expression of Interest fee took effect on 2026-04-07. Alberta now requires a CAD 135 fee to submit a Worker EOI for AAIP worker streams.
- This affects employment-linked pathways that now run through the Worker EOI system, including Alberta Opportunity Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, Tourism and Hospitality Stream, and Alberta Express Entry Stream invitations.
- Current 2026 processing data on the official AAIP processing page shows these streams remain active rather than paused:
- Alberta Opportunity Stream: 3,425 allocation, 911 nominations issued, 2,514 remaining, currently assessing applications received on or before 2026-02-02.
- Rural Renewal Stream: 1,000 allocation, 176 nominations issued, 824 remaining, currently assessing applications received on or before 2026-02-27.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream: 150 allocation, 45 nominations issued, 105 remaining, currently assessing applications received on or before 2026-03-10.

## What Did Not Change
- No official Alberta source checked in this run showed a new intake pause or reopening for the main lower-barrier or employment-linked worker pathways.
- Alberta still uses the invitation-only Worker EOI model for worker streams introduced on 2024-09-30.
- Rural Renewal Stream still requires a job offer from an employer in a designated community and a community endorsement letter.
- Alberta Express Entry Stream still operates by invitation from the federal Express Entry pool rather than direct open intake.

## Relevance To My Situation
- The clearest practical change is cost and friction: there is now a CAD 135 fee just to enter the Worker EOI pool.
- Alberta remains operational for job-linked pathways, especially Alberta Opportunity Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, and Tourism and Hospitality Stream, so Alberta is still relevant if I can secure a qualifying Alberta job offer.
- Rural Renewal and Tourism and Hospitality remain more employment-driven than profile-driven, which can still matter if my strongest route is getting hired first rather than relying on a high CRS score.
