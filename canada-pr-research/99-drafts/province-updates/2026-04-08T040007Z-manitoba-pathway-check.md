## What Changed
- No new Manitoba pathway rule change or new draw was posted on the official Manitoba immigration site between the latest visible notice on 2026-03-26 and this check on 2026-04-08.
- The most recent official draw remains Expression of Interest Draw #267 on 2026-03-26, with 14 LAAs issued only to Skilled Worker candidates who declared a direct MPNP invitation under a strategic recruitment initiative.
- The most recent operational change still visible is the 2026-03-02 contact-process update: Manitoba shifted applicant inquiries from email to webforms and stated it will not respond to contact-form requests for EOI edits, EOI cancellation/re-submission handling, or LAA deadline extensions.

## What Did Not Change
- Skilled Worker Overseas still requires a Manitoba connection through Manitoba Support, Manitoba Experience, or a direct MPNP invitation under a strategic recruitment initiative, plus at least 60 assessment points.
- Skilled Worker in Manitoba still requires ongoing Manitoba employment, typically after at least six months of continuous full-time work with the Manitoba employer.
- The official 2026 draw pattern still looks highly targeted rather than broad-based. The January 15, January 29, February 12, February 26, March 12, and March 26, 2026 notices all show Skilled Worker invitations tied to strategic recruitment initiatives, not a general open overseas draw.
- Manitoba still presents the Skilled Worker Stream as employer- and labour-market-driven.
- Employer Direct Initiative still appears active as the main overseas or outside-Canada employer-linked route, while the Temporary Resident Retention Pilot remains a separate retention path for workers already employed in Manitoba.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For an overseas applicant, this is still a hard signal that Manitoba is not currently showing a broad, easy-access Skilled Worker Overseas invitation pattern. The official route still depends on a Manitoba connection, and recent draws are being filtered through strategic recruitment invitations.
- For work-linked planning, Manitoba continues to favor employer-linked pathways. That makes a real employer connection, recruitment initiative, or Manitoba-based work history more important than simply having an EOI profile.
- If the practical plan is a lower-barrier PR path from outside Canada, Manitoba still looks selective rather than open as of 2026-04-08.
