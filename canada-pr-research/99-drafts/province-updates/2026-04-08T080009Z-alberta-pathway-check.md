## What Changed
- AAIP now requires a `$135` fee to submit a `Worker Expression of Interest (EOI)`, effective `2026-04-07`. This affects worker-stream access before invitation and adds a new upfront cost even for candidates who are not selected.
- Alberta government news on `2026-04-01` introduced the proposed `Immigration Oversight Act`, focused on employer, recruiter, and consultant oversight. This is not a direct AAIP stream eligibility change yet, but it is relevant to employment-linked pathways because it signals tighter scrutiny around job offers and recruitment activity.

## What Did Not Change
- AAIP worker streams still use the invitation-only `Worker EOI` model introduced on `2024-09-30`.
- The main employment-linked / lower-barrier streams checked in this run still appear open on their official pages, with no confirmed intake pause or reopening announcement found in this check:
- `Alberta Opportunity Stream`
- `Rural Renewal Stream`
- `Tourism and Hospitality Stream`
- Rural Renewal still requires community designation and candidate endorsement from a designated community.
- Alberta Opportunity Stream still targets people already living and working in Alberta with a qualifying Alberta job offer.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream still remains a narrow employer-linked route for workers already in Alberta in eligible tourism and hospitality roles.

## Relevance To My Situation
- The new `Worker EOI` fee is a real new barrier, but it is relatively small compared with the overall cost of a move. The bigger issue is that Alberta still mainly favors people who already have Alberta employment, Alberta work status, or a rural community endorsement.
- For a lower-barrier strategy, `Rural Renewal Stream` remains the most notable Alberta option because it can connect nomination to smaller-community employer demand, but it still depends on getting both a qualifying job and community endorsement.
- `Alberta Opportunity Stream` and `Tourism and Hospitality Stream` still look more realistic after securing Alberta-based work first, not as direct overseas-access pathways.
