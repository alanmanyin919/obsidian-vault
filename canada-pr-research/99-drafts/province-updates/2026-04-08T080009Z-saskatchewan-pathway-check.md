## What Changed
- No new Saskatchewan pathway announcement was found on official pages for this check dated 2026-04-08.
- A still-recent practical change is now fully in effect: as of 2026-04-01, the SINP $500 application fee and $250 second review fee apply to all worker applicants in all categories.
- Current 2026 operating rules remain active: Saskatchewan is prioritizing health care, agriculture, skilled trades, mining, manufacturing, energy, and technology, while retail, trucking, and accommodation/food services remain capped sectors with intake windows and a last-6-months-of-work-permit rule.

## What Did Not Change
- Saskatchewan still shows pathways for applicants without a Saskatchewan job offer through International Skilled Worker: Saskatchewan Express Entry and Occupations In-Demand, with the usual 60-point minimum still shown on the eligibility page.
- Saskatchewan still lists employment-linked options for workers and students, including Employment Offer, Agriculture Talent, Health Talent, and Innovation and Tech Talent.
- Capped-sector intake rules still show six 2026 intake windows, and applicants in capped sectors are still restricted to applying only when they have six months or less left on their work permit.
- No official page reviewed in this run showed a new reopening, closure, or eligibility expansion specifically aimed at overseas low-barrier applicants.

## Relevance To My Situation
- Saskatchewan remains more realistic if the plan involves getting into Canada first and then using an employer-linked pathway, because the province is explicitly prioritizing in-Canada temporary residents and several sectors now have tighter intake controls.
- For a profile targeting lower-barrier work, retail, trucking, and accommodation/food services are still usable but less attractive than before because of sector caps, intake windows, and the six-month work-permit timing rule.
- If the target role can move into a priority sector such as health support, trades-adjacent work, manufacturing, energy, or tech-supporting roles that fit Saskatchewan criteria, Saskatchewan stays more feasible than capped-sector service work.
- The new province-wide worker application fee slightly increases the cost of using Saskatchewan as a pathway.
