## What Changed
- Alberta posted a new AAIP update dated April 7, 2026: a `CAD 135` Worker Expression of Interest fee now applies to worker-stream EOIs, including Alberta Opportunity Stream, Tourism and Hospitality Stream, and Rural Renewal Stream.
- Alberta also lists a March 11, 2026 AAIP update saying Worker EOIs submitted on or after February 25, 2026 must include wage and hours-of-work details for candidates with an Alberta job offer; AAIP says these factors may be considered in future draws.
- Alberta’s processing page now shows March 2026 draw activity continuing, including Rural Renewal Stream and other worker-stream draws, which indicates the tracked pathways are active rather than paused.

## What Did Not Change
- I did not find a newly announced intake pause or reopening for Alberta Opportunity Stream, Tourism and Hospitality Stream, or Rural Renewal Stream on the official pages checked.
- I did not find a new stream-specific eligibility rewrite for Alberta Opportunity Stream or Tourism and Hospitality Stream in this run.
- The main structural limits already in place still appear to remain: worker streams require invitation through the Worker EOI system, Alberta Opportunity Stream and Tourism and Hospitality Stream remain employment-linked and Alberta-based, and Rural Renewal Stream still depends on a designated-community endorsement plus a qualifying job offer.
- The older Rural Renewal Stream tightening effective January 1, 2026 still appears to be the main recent eligibility change for lower-barrier Alberta pathways.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For lower-barrier Alberta options, the practical routes still look mostly employer-linked rather than open-entry. That keeps Rural Renewal Stream and Tourism and Hospitality Stream relevant only if there is a real job offer and, for Rural Renewal Stream, community endorsement.
- The new `CAD 135` EOI fee raises the cost of entering or re-entering the worker-stream pool and makes repeated EOI resubmissions a bit more expensive.
- The added wage and hours fields suggest Alberta may weigh job quality more explicitly in future selections, which could matter if comparing lower-wage hospitality roles against stronger full-time offers.

## Uncertainties

- This check confirms the current official pages as of April 8, 2026, but not whether these changes were already captured in a prior draft run.
