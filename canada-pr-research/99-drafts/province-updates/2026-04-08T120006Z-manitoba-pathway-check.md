## What Changed
- Manitoba posted newer Expression of Interest draws on March 12, 2026 and March 26, 2026. Both draws were limited to Skilled Worker in Manitoba or Skilled Worker Overseas profiles that had already been directly invited under strategic recruitment initiatives, rather than broad general EOI selection.
- The most recent draw visible on the official news page is Draw #267 dated March 26, 2026, with 14 Letters of Advice to Apply. The previous draw, #266 dated March 12, 2026, issued 46 Letters of Advice to Apply. This suggests continued low-volume, targeted selection for this route.
- Manitoba announced a process change effective March 2, 2026: MPNP moved applicant/public inquiries away from email and to webforms/contact forms, and stated it would not respond to several request types by email or webform.
- Manitoba also announced a February 4, 2026 change to the Temporary Resident Retention Pilot: hospitality and food services were removed from eligible sectors and some skilled trades were added. This is relevant to work-linked Manitoba options, though it is not an overseas pathway.

## What Did Not Change
- The core EOI framework still appears unchanged on the official EOI page: EOIs remain valid for one year, candidates are ranked in a pool, and Manitoba keeps discretion to limit or exclude occupations from draws.
- Skilled Worker Overseas still appears to operate through Manitoba connection or strategic recruitment logic rather than an open, frequent, broad-based overseas draw pattern.
- The Employer Direct Initiative still presents itself as a Manitoba employer-led overseas recruitment route, with employers required to show exhausted local recruitment, Manitoba operations, revenue thresholds, and ongoing Job Bank advertising.

## Relevance To My Situation
- This remains a difficult province to rely on as a generic overseas applicant without a Manitoba-specific connection, direct strategic recruitment invitation, or employer-led route.
- The current draw pattern favors candidates already pulled into Manitoba's strategic channels, not passive EOI candidates waiting for a broad overseas draw.
- If Manitoba stays on the table, the more realistic angle appears to be work-linked pathways such as an employer-supported route or a Manitoba-specific recruitment initiative, rather than expecting a standard overseas EOI invitation.

## Uncertainties
- I did not find a separate Manitoba government news release outside the official Manitoba Immigration site that added newer pathway-rule detail beyond what is already published there.
- The official news page excerpt confirms the draw pattern and counts, but a deeper trend check would require reviewing more individual draw pages or monthly data tables over a longer period.
- It is unclear from this check alone whether March 26, 2026 was the latest draw because no April 2026 draw was visible on the official news page at the time of checking.

## Suggested Next Step
- Keep Manitoba in watch mode rather than treating it as a primary passive-overseas option, and on the next scheduled check verify whether a new April 2026 draw appears or whether Manitoba reopens any broader non-strategic selection pattern.
