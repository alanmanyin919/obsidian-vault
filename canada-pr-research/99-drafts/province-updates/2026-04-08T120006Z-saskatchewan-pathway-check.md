## What Changed
- No new Saskatchewan pathway announcement was found on official pages during this 2026-04-08 check.
- The active 2026 framework still shows these already-in-force changes: SINP worker fees expanded on 2026-04-01 to $500 application fee plus $250 second-review fee across worker categories.
- The 2026 SINP allocation is now 4,761, with at least 50 per cent reserved for priority sectors: healthcare, agriculture, skilled trades, mining, manufacturing, energy, and technology.
- Capped sectors remain tightly controlled in 2026: accommodation and food services 15 per cent, trucking 5 per cent, retail trade 5 per cent. For capped sectors, employers can only submit during intake windows and only for candidates with six months or less left on their work permit.
- Saskatchewan still shows no scheduled International Skilled Worker EOI draws for no-job-offer pathways.

## What Did Not Change
- Saskatchewan still prioritizes employer-linked pathways over broad no-job-offer access.
- Overseas support still remains much stronger for priority sectors than for general occupations.
- OID and Saskatchewan Express Entry still depend on occupation eligibility and current labour-market demand, and there is still no scheduled EOI draw timeline posted.
- Entrepreneur, International Graduate Entrepreneur, and Farm Owner/Operator pathways remain permanently closed.
- The Innovation and Tech Talent Pathway remains available with a Saskatchewan job offer in listed tech or innovation occupations.

## Relevance To My Situation

- If you are studying or working in Saskatchewan, the province is still reserving 750 nominations for Saskatchewan post-secondary graduates with job offers in priority sectors, which improves practicality for that narrower profile.
