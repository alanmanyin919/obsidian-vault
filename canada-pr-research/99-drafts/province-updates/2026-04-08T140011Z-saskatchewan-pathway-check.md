## What Did Not Change
- The SINP FAQ still shows the 2026 initial nomination allocation at 4,761, with no current federal rule requiring a fixed share of nominees to already be temporary residents in Canada.
- The April 1, 2026 fee expansion is still live across worker categories: $500 application fee plus $250 second-review fee.
- Saskatchewan still reserves at least 50 per cent of 2026 nominations for priority sectors: healthcare, agriculture, skilled trades, mining, manufacturing, energy, and technology.
- Saskatchewan still caps accommodation and food services at 15 per cent, trucking at 5 per cent, and retail trade at 5 per cent of 2026 nominations, with intake-window controls for those sectors.
- The processing page still shows fast current posted times for employer-linked routes in Q1 2026: Employment Offer 3 weeks, Tech Talent 2 weeks, Agriculture Talent 3 weeks, Health Talent 2 weeks, and Existing Work Permit 3 weeks.
- Saskatchewan still treats employer-backed routes as the practical path. The March 27, 2025 official change remains in force: Saskatchewan Express Entry and Occupations In-Demand applications without a Saskatchewan-based job offer are returned.
- Entrepreneur, International Graduate Entrepreneur, and Farm pathways remain permanently closed.
