## What Did Not Change
- The SINP FAQ still shows the 2026 initial nomination allocation at 4,761, with at least 50 per cent reserved for priority sectors and 750 of those spots reserved for Saskatchewan graduates working in priority sectors.
- The April 1, 2026 fee expansion is still live across worker categories: $500 application fee plus $250 second-review fee.
- Saskatchewan still limits capped sectors to accommodation and food services, trucking, and retail trade, with intake-window controls and a rule that candidates in those sectors must be within the last six months of their work permit validity.
- The processing statistics page still shows fast posted Q1 2026 processing times for employer-linked routes: Employment Offer 3 weeks, Tech Talent 2 weeks, Agriculture Talent 3 weeks, Health Talent 2 weeks, and Existing Work Permit 3 weeks.
- Saskatchewan still treats employer-backed routes as the practical path. The March 27, 2025 program change remains in effect: Saskatchewan Express Entry and Occupations In-Demand applications without a Saskatchewan job offer are returned.
- The SINP FAQ still limits many open-work-permit holders to a narrower set of pathways. PGWP holders remain restricted to Student, Health Talent, Agriculture Talent, Innovation and Tech Talent, and International Skilled Worker: Employment Offer. Spousal open work permit holders may be eligible for Agriculture Talent, Health Talent, Innovation and Tech Talent, and Employment Offer.
- Entrepreneur, International Graduate Entrepreneur, and Farm pathways remain permanently closed.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For this profile, Saskatchewan still does not look like a dependable no-job-offer province. The practical route remains employer-linked, not speculative.
- If the plan depends on arriving on an open work permit and then using a broad Saskatchewan pathway later, the current rules still make that weaker than a direct Saskatchewan job-offer-first strategy.
- Saskatchewan remains more relevant if the target can be a Saskatchewan employer-backed role in a priority sector or an eligible tech occupation. It remains less attractive if the realistic first jobs are general admin, retail, food service, or other roles that do not clearly map to a strong employer-supported pathway.
