## What Changed
- No new official Manitoba pathway-rule change was found on this check.
- The latest visible MPNP draw notice remains Expression of Interest Draw #267 dated March 26, 2026.
- Recent draw pattern still appears highly targeted rather than broad: the March 12 and March 26, 2026 notices limited Skilled Worker consideration to candidates directly invited under strategic recruitment initiatives or the temporary public policy route.

## What Did Not Change
- Skilled Worker Stream still shows the same two main pathways: Skilled Worker in Manitoba and Skilled Worker Overseas.
- Skilled Worker Overseas still requires an established Manitoba connection through family/friends, previous Manitoba education or work experience, or a direct MPNP strategic recruitment invitation.
- The EOI page still says draws are regular, profile validity is one year, and Manitoba may limit or exclude occupations from draws based on inventory or labour-market outlook.
- Manitoba’s work-linked orientation did not appear to change: current draws and pathway descriptions still favour Manitoba-based employment links or direct recruitment-linked invitations over open overseas selection.

## Relevance To My Situation
- This remains a difficult province for a pure overseas applicant without a strong Manitoba connection.
- Manitoba still looks more realistic if access comes through a Manitoba employer, Manitoba work experience, or a direct strategic recruitment initiative rather than a general overseas EOI.
- The recent draw pattern is still relevant for work-linked routes because invitations continue to be concentrated in employer services, francophone, ethnocultural, regional community, and temporary-policy-linked channels instead of broad open draws.

## Uncertainties
- As of this check, the MPNP Monthly Data 2026 page only showed January and February totals, so March 2026 program-volume data was not yet visible there.
- Manitoba’s temporary public policy page says IRCC would accept applications under that policy until December 31, 2025, but March 2026 draw notices still include a small number of TPP-related invitations. It is unclear from the public pages whether these reflect residual processing from earlier eligible candidates or another administrative mechanism.
- A March 2, 2026 contact-process change was posted officially, but it appears administrative rather than a pathway eligibility change.
