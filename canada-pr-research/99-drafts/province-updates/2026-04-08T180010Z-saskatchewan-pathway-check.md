## What Changed
- No brand-new Saskatchewan pathway announcement was found on 2026-04-08, but the current official 2026 rules now in force are materially different from the older 2025 setup.
- Effective 2026-04-01, Saskatchewan extended the `CAD 500` application fee and `CAD 250` second-review fee to all worker applicants in all categories.
- Saskatchewan’s 2026 SINP allocation is listed as `4,761`, and the FAQ says the 2025 federal rule requiring `75%` of nominees to already be temporary residents in Canada no longer applies in 2026.
- Saskatchewan formally structured 2026 nominations around sector priorities: at least `50%` for priority sectors, up to `25%` for capped sectors, and `750` reserved spaces for Saskatchewan graduates working in priority sectors.
- The Innovation and Tech Talent Pathway states that, as of `2026-01-01`, it expanded to `32` eligible high-skilled occupations.
- Official processing/intake pages now show 2026 capped-sector intake mechanics in operation, including six intake windows and the rule that capped-sector candidates generally need `6 months or less` left on their work permit to be submitted.

## What Did Not Change
- No new Saskatchewan government release today was found announcing an additional SINP pathway opening, closure, or occupation-list overhaul beyond the already-published 2025 to 2026 changes.
- Saskatchewan Express Entry and Occupations In-Demand still exist on the site for applicants without job offers, but the official EOI page still says there are no scheduled EOI draws at this time.
- Overseas candidates are still effectively prioritized only in priority sectors; outside those sectors, Saskatchewan remains much more employer-linked and in-Canada focused.
- Entrepreneur, International Graduate Entrepreneur, and Farm Owner/Operator pathways remain closed.
- Spousal open work permit holders remain restricted from many SINP routes, with access mainly preserved through Health Talent, Agriculture Talent, Innovation and Tech Talent, and International Skilled Worker: Employment Offer.

## Relevance To My Situation
- If this profile is still aiming for lower-barrier support or service work, Saskatchewan remains harder than before in retail, food service, and similar capped sectors because those sectors have strict caps, intake windows, and work-permit-timing limits.
- If this profile can target a Saskatchewan employer in tech, IT support, user support, QA/testing, or another eligible tech occupation, Saskatchewan looks more practical than it did under the 2025 allocation cut because the 2026 rules explicitly prioritize technology and the Tech Talent Pathway has expanded occupation coverage.
- If this profile does not already have Saskatchewan study history or a Saskatchewan job offer, the province is still not an easy standalone no-offer pathway right now because the OID/EE pages remain open in theory but the EOI system still shows no scheduled draws.
- The new universal application fee makes Saskatchewan slightly more expensive to test as a backup option.
