## What Changed
- No new Manitoba pathway-rule or draw notice was found on 2026-04-08. This is a dated no-change check.
- The latest official draw still on record is Draw #267 dated 2026-03-26. It issued 14 LAAs only to candidates in the Skilled Worker Stream who had been directly invited under strategic recruitment initiatives, including Employer Services, Francophone, Ethnocultural, and TPP-linked cases.
- The recent 2026 draw pattern still shows restricted, invitation-led selection rather than broad general EOI draws for overseas applicants. Recent Skilled Worker draw volumes were 72 on 2026-02-26, 46 on 2026-03-12, and 14 on 2026-03-26.
- The most recent work-linked pathway rule change still in effect is the TRRP update effective 2026-02-04: hospitality and food services is no longer an eligible sector, while additional skilled trades were added.
- The most recent process change still in effect is the 2026-03-02 contact-process update: MPNP moved general inquiries to webforms and said it would no longer respond to new email inquiries.

## What Did Not Change
- The Skilled Worker Stream still has two pathways: Skilled Worker in Manitoba and Skilled Worker Overseas.
- The Skilled Worker Overseas pathway page still requires an established Manitoba connection, such as support from family or friends, previous Manitoba work or study, or a direct MPNP invitation through a strategic recruitment initiative.
- No new pause, reopening, or redesign of the Skilled Worker Overseas pathway was posted in the official sources checked.
- No new Manitoba government immigration update was found changing overseas eligibility, Employer Services access, or the Skilled Worker stream after the late-March draw.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For an overseas applicant, Manitoba still does not look like an open general-access route right now. The 2026 draw pattern continues to favor people already pulled in through strategic recruitment or other targeted channels rather than broad overseas EOI selection.
- For a work-linked strategy, Employer Services and other employer-connected or targeted initiatives remain more relevant than relying on a passive Skilled Worker Overseas EOI alone.
- The TRRP change matters if the realistic job plan includes hospitality or food service work in Manitoba, because that pilot no longer supports that sector after 2026-02-04.

## Uncertainties
- The official monthly data page currently showed January and February 2026 data only during this check, so there is not yet a full official March monthly-data release on that page to compare against the March draws.
- Manitoba’s official pages show draw outputs and notices, but they do not clearly state whether broader non-targeted overseas invitations are intentionally paused or simply not being used recently. The current conclusion is based on observed official draw pattern, not on an explicit suspension notice.
- I did not find a separate Manitoba government news release outside the Manitoba Immigration official site adding new pathway changes for this check window.

## Suggested Next Step
- Keep Manitoba on watch, but treat it as a targeted or employer-linked province for now. On the next scheduled check, look for a new draw after 2026-03-26 or any notice changing Skilled Worker Overseas access, Employer Services rules, or TRRP sector eligibility.
