## What Did Not Change
- Saskatchewan still shows a 2026 nomination allocation of `4,761`, with at least `50%` reserved for priority sectors and up to `25%` allocated to capped sectors.
- The capped sectors remain Accommodation and Food Services, Trucking, and Retail Trade, with six intake windows in 2026 and the rule that affected candidates generally need `6 months or less` left on their work permit for intake-based JAF submission.
- The Innovation and Tech Talent Pathway still shows the January 1, 2026 expansion and continues to include tech-adjacent roles such as `22221 User support technicians` and `22222 Information systems testing technicians`, which keeps Saskatchewan more relevant for IT support or QA-type profiles than for general service work.
- Saskatchewan Express Entry and Occupations In-Demand remain visible on the official site, but the March 27, 2025 government release still states that no-job-offer applications under those sub-categories would be returned under the post-pause rules.
- Spousal open work permit holders still appear restricted from many SINP routes, with the FAQ continuing to preserve clearer access mainly through Agriculture Talent, Health Talent, Innovation and Tech Talent, and International Skilled Worker: Employment Offer.
- Entrepreneur, International Graduate Entrepreneur, and Farm Owner/Operator pathways remain closed on the official site.
