## What Changed
- No new meaningful Alberta pathway change was visible on the official AAIP pages or Alberta government immigration news pages checked during this run.
- The newest official AAIP update still appears to be the April 7, 2026 Worker Expression of Interest fee change introducing a $135 WEOI fee.

## What Did Not Change
- Alberta Opportunity Stream still requires a valid work permit plus a full-time Alberta job offer in an eligible occupation, and it still says maintained status or restoration status does not count as qualifying work authorization.
- Rural Renewal Stream still requires a full-time job offer and a valid endorsement letter from a designated rural community, and endorsement letters still remain valid for one year.
- Rural Renewal Stream community designation still reflects yearly community allocations rather than an open unlimited endorsement model.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream still remains tightly employer-linked, including current Alberta work with an approved employer and at least 6 consecutive months of full-time work with that same employer.
- Alberta government immigration news still does not show a newer official announcement after April 1, 2026 that directly changes stream eligibility, pauses intake, or reopens a closed pathway.
