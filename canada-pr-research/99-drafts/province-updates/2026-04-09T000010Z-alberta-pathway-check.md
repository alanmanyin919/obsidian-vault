## What Changed
- No new meaningful Alberta pathway change was visible on the official AAIP pages or Alberta government immigration news pages checked during this run.
- The newest official AAIP update still appears to be the April 7, 2026 Worker Expression of Interest fee change introducing a $135 WEOI fee.
- The AAIP processing page still shows the April 1, 2026 update, with 2026 worker-stream priority focus still directed toward health care, technology, construction, manufacturing, aviation, agriculture, and designated Rural Renewal communities.

## What Did Not Change
- Alberta Opportunity Stream still requires a valid work permit and a full-time Alberta job offer, and it still says maintained status or restoration status does not count as qualifying work authorization.
- Rural Renewal Stream still requires a full-time job offer plus a valid community endorsement letter, and the endorsement letter still remains valid for one year.
- Rural Renewal communities still operate with yearly allocations rather than an open-ended endorsement model.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream still remains tightly employer-linked, including current Alberta work for an approved employer, a valid eligible work permit, and at least 6 consecutive months of full-time work with that same employer.
- Alberta government immigration news still does not show a newer official announcement after April 1, 2026 that directly changes stream eligibility, pauses intake, or reopens a pathway.
