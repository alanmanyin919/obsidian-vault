## What Changed
- No new Alberta pathway rule change, intake pause, or reopening was posted on the official AAIP update pages or Alberta government news pages during this run.
- This run confirmed the current official AAIP worker-stream process still includes the April 7, 2026 Worker Expression of Interest fee change: a $135 fee is now required to submit a Worker EOI for AAIP worker streams.
- The AAIP processing page still shows the April 1, 2026 update that 2026 worker-stream draws and nominations are being prioritized toward health care, technology, construction, manufacturing, aviation, agriculture, and designated Rural Renewal communities.

## What Did Not Change
- Alberta Opportunity Stream still requires a valid work permit both when applying and when AAIP assesses the file; maintained status and restoration status still do not qualify.
- Rural Renewal Stream still requires both a full-time job offer in a designated community and a valid community endorsement letter.
- Rural Renewal communities still operate with yearly endorsement allocations rather than open-ended intake.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream still remains tightly employer-linked: current Alberta work, an eligible work permit, the same approved employer, and at least 6 consecutive months of full-time work are still required.
- Alberta government news still does not show a newer announcement after April 1, 2026 that directly changes stream eligibility or opens an easier low-barrier pathway.

## Relevance To My Situation
- Alberta still looks more realistic through employer-linked routes than through any easy independent pathway.
- The Rural Renewal Stream remains the closest Alberta option to a lower-barrier route, but only if a designated community endorsement and real job offer can be secured.
- The new $135 Worker EOI fee raises the cost of attempting Alberta worker-stream access, even before a full application is submitted.

## Uncertainties
- AAIP can change actual selection behavior through Worker EOI draws without immediately rewriting stream eligibility pages.
- The April 1, 2026 immigration oversight bill could later affect employer or recruiter access in practice, but this run did not show a direct stream-rule change yet.
- Some Alberta pages are updated outside the main AAIP updates page, so operational changes can appear first on process pages rather than in news or updates summaries.
