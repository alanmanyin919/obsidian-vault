## What Changed
- No new official Alberta pathway change, intake pause, or reopening was posted during this check.
- The latest official items still in force are unchanged from the prior run: the Rural Renewal Stream changes effective January 1, 2026, the April 1, 2026 processing-priority update, and the April 7, 2026 $135 Worker Expression of Interest fee for AAIP worker streams.

## What Did Not Change
- Alberta Opportunity Stream still requires a valid work permit at both application and assessment stages, and maintained status or restoration status still does not qualify.
- Rural Renewal Stream still requires a full-time job offer in a designated community plus a valid Endorsement of Candidate letter, and designated communities still work from yearly endorsement allocations.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream still remains tightly employer-linked: current Alberta employment, an eligible work permit, the same approved employer, and at least 6 consecutive months of full-time work are still required.
- The AAIP processing page still says 2026 worker-stream draws and nominations are being prioritized toward health care, technology, construction, manufacturing, aviation, agriculture, and designated Rural Renewal communities.
- Alberta government news still does not show a newer announcement after the April 1, 2026 immigration oversight release that directly changes stream eligibility or opens an easier lower-barrier pathway.

## Relevance To My Situation
- Alberta still looks more viable through employer-linked routes than through an easier independent pathway.
- Rural Renewal Stream remains the closest Alberta option to a lower-barrier route, but it still depends on getting both a real job offer and community endorsement.
- The Worker EOI fee and 2026 sector-priority approach continue to make Alberta more selective and somewhat more expensive to try.

## Uncertainties
- AAIP can shift practical access through Worker EOI selection behavior before it makes another major eligibility-page change.
- The April 1, 2026 oversight legislation may later affect employer or recruiter access in practice, but this run did not show a direct stream-rule change yet.
- Alberta government news search results were sparse, so an operational update could appear first on an AAIP process page rather than as a dedicated news release.
