## What Changed
- No new April 2026 Manitoba pathway-rule update or new draw notice was visible on the official Manitoba immigration site at the time of this check.
- The latest official draw still visible was EOI Draw #267 dated 2026-03-26. It issued 14 Letters of Advice to Apply and continued the same targeted pattern: only Skilled Worker in Manitoba or Skilled Worker Overseas candidates directly invited under a strategic recruitment initiative, plus a small number tied to the temporary public policy stream.
- No new sign appeared that Manitoba has reopened broad general Skilled Worker Overseas selection from the full EOI pool.

## What Did Not Change
- Manitoba draw activity still appears narrowly targeted rather than broad score-cutoff draws open to all EOI candidates.
- Overseas-oriented access still appears to run mainly through strategic recruitment or employer-linked channels. The Employer Direct Initiative remains positioned as the main overseas recruitment route for Manitoba employers.
- Work-linked retention remains focused on temporary residents already employed in Manitoba through the Temporary Resident Retention Pilot, and the February 4, 2026 sector narrowing remains the latest visible rule-level change there.
- The temporary public policy support-letter route for prospective nominees remains closed to new applications after 2025-12-15, and IRCC acceptance under that measure ended on 2025-12-31.
- The March 2, 2026 MPNP contact-process change still appears administrative only, with no visible eligibility expansion for applicants.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For an overseas applicant without a Manitoba connection, this check still does not show an easier direct-access Manitoba pathway opening up.
- Manitoba continues to look more realistic if the plan includes a Manitoba employer relationship, recruitment through Employer Services, or another strategic recruitment invitation channel.
- For work-linked planning inside Manitoba, the TRRP still matters, but it is narrower after the February 2026 sector change and is not a general overseas route.

## Uncertainties

The newer dedicated TRRP page appears more authoritative, but the FAQ inconsistency should be watched.
