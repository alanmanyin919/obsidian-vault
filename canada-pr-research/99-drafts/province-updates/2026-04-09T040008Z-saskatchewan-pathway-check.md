## What Did Not Change
- The SINP FAQ still shows an initial 2026 nomination allocation of `4,761`, with at least `50%` reserved for priority sectors and up to `25%` reserved for capped sectors, and it still says extra nominations may become available later in 2026 at IRCC's discretion.
- The April 1, 2026 worker-fee expansion still appears active: `CAD 500` application fee and `CAD 250` second-review fee across worker categories.
- Capped sectors still remain Accommodation and Food Services, Retail Trade, and Trucking, and the processing page still says employers in those sectors can submit only during the 2026 intake windows and only for candidates with `6 months or less` left on their work permit.
- The processing statistics page still shows Quarter 1, 2026 processing times of `3 weeks` for Employment Offer, `2 weeks` for Tech Talent, `3 weeks` for Agriculture Talent, `2 weeks` for Health Talent, and `4 weeks` for Job Approval Forms.
- The official EOI page still says there are no scheduled EOI draws at this time for the no-job-offer International Skilled Worker route.
- The Innovation and Tech Talent Pathway still includes profile-relevant NOCs such as `22221` User support technicians, `22222` Information systems testing technicians, `21230` Computer systems developers and programmers, `21232` Software developers and programmers, and `21234` Web developers and programmers.
- Entrepreneur and farm pathways still remain permanently closed.

## Relevance To My Situation
