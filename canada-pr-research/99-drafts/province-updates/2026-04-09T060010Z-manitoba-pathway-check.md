## What Changed
- No new April 2026 Manitoba pathway-rule notice was posted on the official pages checked as of 2026-04-09T060010Z.
- The latest draw visible in the official EOI archive is Draw #267 dated 2026-03-26. It issued 14 Letters of Advice to Apply, all under strategic recruitment initiatives, with no general high-volume overseas draw pattern visible.
- Manitoba’s March 2, 2026 contact-process change is now in force: MPNP moved applicants away from email to webforms and states it will not respond to many routine EOI or application-status requests by email/webform.
- A work-linked rule change already in force from February 4, 2026 remains relevant: under the Temporary Resident Retention Pilot, hospitality and food services was removed as an eligible sector, while several skilled trades occupations were added.
- The newer employer-linked structure still appears active: Employer Direct Initiative is positioned for overseas or outside-Canada recruitment, while the Temporary Resident Retention Pilot is for retaining workers already employed in Manitoba.

## What Did Not Change
- Skilled Worker Overseas still requires a Manitoba connection through family/friend support, past Manitoba study/work, or a direct MPNP strategic recruitment invitation.
- Skilled Worker in Manitoba still centers on ongoing Manitoba employment and generally requires at least six months of continuous full-time work with the Manitoba employer before application; graduates from another Canadian province still need at least one year with the Manitoba employer.
- Manitoba still appears to be using strategic recruitment initiatives heavily in 2026 draws rather than broad open overseas invitations.
- The 2026 monthly data page still shows 6,239 total nominations allocated and 230 Skilled Worker LAAs issued across January and February combined.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For an overseas applicant, Manitoba still does not look like an easy direct-entry option unless there is a genuine Manitoba connection or access to a strategic recruitment route.
- For a work-linked route, Manitoba remains more realistic if a Manitoba employer relationship can be built first, especially through in-province employment retention or an employer-backed recruitment path.
- If the target job family overlaps with one of the newly included skilled trades, the Temporary Resident Retention Pilot change may improve Manitoba usefulness. If the plan depends on hospitality or food service, this February 2026 change makes Manitoba less useful.
- The March 2026 contact-rule change means slower or more constrained clarification paths; applicants likely need to rely more on published rules and use the exact webform channel.

## Uncertainties
- I did not find a new April 2026 policy bulletin beyond the March 26, 2026 draw archive entry, but Manitoba could post a new notice after this check time.
- The monthly data page says Manitoba was allocated 6,239 nominations for 2026, while an October 8, 2025 notice says 6,239 was the revised 2025 total. I am treating the 2026 monthly data page as the current official figure, but this cross-page inconsistency is worth monitoring.
- Draw archives show invitation volumes and channels, but they do not fully reveal internal selection priorities beyond the published categories.
