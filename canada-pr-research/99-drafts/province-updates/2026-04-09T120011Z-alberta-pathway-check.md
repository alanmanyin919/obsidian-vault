## What Changed
- AAIP posted a new official update dated April 7, 2026: a `Worker Expression of Interest` now costs `$135` to submit. This applies across AAIP worker streams, which matters for Alberta Opportunity Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, Tourism and Hospitality Stream, and Alberta Express Entry-linked worker intake.
- AAIP also posted a March 11, 2026 update stating that Worker EOIs submitted on or after February 25, 2026 must include wage and hours-of-work information when the candidate has an Alberta job offer. AAIP says missing this information can exclude a profile from future draws.
- Alberta is still actively inviting candidates in employment-linked streams rather than pausing them. The official processing page shows 2026 draws for Alberta Opportunity Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, and Tourism and Hospitality Stream, so these pathways appear open and functioning rather than paused.
- Alberta’s 2026 processing guidance says worker-stream draws and nominations are being prioritized toward key sectors and designated Rural Renewal communities. That makes generic low-barrier pathways less broad than they may have looked before.

## What Did Not Change
- I did not find a new intake pause or reopening notice for Alberta Opportunity Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, or Tourism and Hospitality Stream in the official sources checked.
- Alberta Opportunity Stream still requires the applicant to already be living and working full-time in Alberta with a qualifying Alberta job offer and valid work authorization.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream still appears to be a narrow employment-linked pathway: full-time work in Alberta with an approved tourism and hospitality employer, an eligible occupation, a valid work permit, and at least 6 consecutive months of qualifying work experience.
- Rural Renewal Stream still remains employer-and-community-linked. Candidates still need a job offer in a designated community and a valid community endorsement letter before moving through the AAIP worker EOI process.

## Relevance To My Situation
- Alberta did not become easier in a broad way during this check. The clearest current worker routes are still employment-linked routes inside Alberta or through a designated rural community.
- For a lower-barrier strategy, Rural Renewal Stream may still be the most relevant Alberta lane if a real employer and designated community back the application, but Alberta tightened this stream in 2026 and is managing it more strategically.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream remains a concrete lower-skill route only for people already working in Alberta in that sector with the right employer and work permit setup.
- The new `$135` Worker EOI fee and the added wage/hours fields raise the cost and precision required to stay competitive in Alberta’s worker-stream pool.
- Alberta’s stated 2026 priority sectors do not clearly favor general office support or other broad entry-level non-priority occupations unless they are tied to a designated rural community or another priority need.

## Uncertainties
- I did not find a separate Alberta government news release about these April 2026 AAIP changes beyond the official AAIP update and processing pages, so the AAIP pages themselves are the main authority for this check.
- AAIP says draws are not regularly scheduled and does not disclose full draw parameters, so it is not possible to tell from official sources alone how competitive a specific lower-barrier occupation currently is.
- Alberta says its listed 2026 priority sectors are not exhaustive, so some occupations outside the named sectors may still receive invitations.
