## What Changed
- AAIP posted a new Worker Expression of Interest fee. Effective April 7, 2026, submitting a Worker EOI now costs $135. This affects access to all worker streams, including Alberta Opportunity, Rural Renewal, and Tourism and Hospitality.
- AAIP posted a Worker EOI system update dated March 11, 2026. Worker EOIs submitted on or after February 25, 2026 must include wage and hours information for candidates with an Alberta job offer, and AAIP says missing this information may exclude candidates from future draws.
- The latest Alberta government immigration news item found was Bill 26, the Immigration Oversight Act, introduced on April 1, 2026. If passed, it would create employer registry and recruiter / consultant licensing rules. This is not a direct stream eligibility change yet, but it is relevant to employment-linked pathways because it targets employer and recruiter conduct around foreign workers.
- The most recent stream-specific rule change still visible on the official AAIP updates page is the Rural Renewal Stream update announced November 18, 2025 and effective January 1, 2026: endorsement allocation limits per designated community, one-year validity for endorsement letters, a TEER-based endorsement model, and a valid-work-permit requirement for foreign nationals already in Canada at application and assessment.

## What Did Not Change
- I did not find a newly posted intake pause or reopening for Alberta Opportunity Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, or Tourism and Hospitality Stream on the official AAIP pages checked.
- I did not find a newly posted eligibility expansion for lower-barrier worker routes such as Tourism and Hospitality Stream or Alberta Opportunity Stream during this check.
- Alberta still shows active 2026 processing allocations for the main employment-linked streams on the processing page, including Alberta Opportunity Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, and Tourism and Hospitality Stream.
- Rural Renewal Stream still remains relevant to candidates outside Canada as well as temporary workers in Canada, but it still requires a designated-community endorsement and then AAIP invitation through the Worker EOI system.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For a lower-barrier or employment-linked route, the biggest practical change in this check is the new $135 Worker EOI fee. Alberta is no longer a no-cost wait-and-see option at the EOI stage.
- If targeting an Alberta employer-linked route, complete wage and hours details now matter in the EOI system and may affect draw visibility for job-offer candidates.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream still appears to be a narrow route for people already working full-time in Alberta for an approved employer in an eligible occupation, so it does not look easier for an out-of-province or overseas applicant from this check alone.
- Rural Renewal remains potentially useful only where there is a concrete designated-community employer path; the January 1, 2026 tightening means community capacity and endorsement availability matter more than before.
