## What Did Not Change
- Skilled Worker Overseas still requires a Manitoba connection such as Manitoba support, prior Manitoba experience, or a direct MPNP invitation through a strategic recruitment initiative.
- Skilled Worker in Manitoba still remains a work-linked route centered on a long-term full-time Manitoba job offer and established employment relationship.
- The draw archive still shows Manitoba using invitation-limited skilled worker draws rather than broad general overseas draws. Recent 2026 draw sizes visible on the archive remain 72 on February 26, 46 on March 12, and 14 on March 26.
- The temporary public policy for prospective PNP candidates remains concluded; Manitoba’s official notice still says new support-letter applications closed on December 16, 2025.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For an overseas applicant, this check does not show any new easier opening into Manitoba. The official structure still points to Skilled Worker Overseas being realistic mainly when there is a real Manitoba connection or direct strategic-recruitment invitation.
- For work-linked routes, Manitoba still looks more concrete if the path is employer-connected. But the Temporary Resident Retention Pilot is for temporary residents already working in Manitoba, so it does not directly help a person applying from overseas.
- The recent skilled worker draw pattern is still narrow and invitation-based, which weakens Manitoba as a broad open pathway for someone without Manitoba work history or a Manitoba-linked invitation channel.
