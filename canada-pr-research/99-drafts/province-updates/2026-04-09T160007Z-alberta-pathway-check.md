## What Changed
- No new official Alberta pathway update was posted during this check window that changed stream eligibility, announced a new intake pause, or announced a reopening.
- The most recent official AAIP changes still visible are unchanged from the prior check: the new `$135` Worker Expression of Interest fee took effect on April 7, 2026, and AAIP's March 11, 2026 WEOI update says candidates with an Alberta job offer must include wage and hours-of-work information or they may be excluded from future draws.

## What Did Not Change
- Alberta worker streams are still operating through the Worker EOI invitation system rather than direct open intake.
- I did not find a new pause or reopening notice for Alberta Opportunity Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, or Tourism and Hospitality Stream.
- Alberta's processing page still shows active 2026 invitations and available nomination spaces for Alberta Opportunity Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, and Tourism and Hospitality Stream.
- Alberta's 2026 worker-stream priority focus still points toward key sectors and designated Rural Renewal communities, which means lower-barrier options remain mostly employer-linked rather than broadly open.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream still appears limited to people already working in Alberta for an approved employer in an eligible occupation with the required work permit and qualifying work history.
- Rural Renewal Stream still appears to require both a qualifying job offer and designated-community endorsement, so it remains concrete only when an employer and community are already in place.

## Relevance To My Situation
- This check did not make Alberta easier in a broad way for a lower-barrier applicant.
- Alberta still looks most usable through employment-linked routes, especially Rural Renewal if a designated community and employer are secured, or Tourism and Hospitality if already working in Alberta under the stream's narrow conditions.
- The April 7 WEOI fee and the added wage/hours detail keep Alberta's process more controlled and less forgiving for weak or incomplete profiles.

## Uncertainties
- Alberta does not publish full draw-selection logic, so official pages do not show how competitive a specific lower-barrier occupation is at this moment.
- The April 1, 2026 Alberta immigration oversight news item is policy-direction news, but it does not yet change AAIP stream eligibility on its face; later regulations could still matter for employer-linked pathways.

## Suggested Next Step
- Keep Alberta on the watchlist as an employer-linked option and check next for any new AAIP draw pattern, Rural Renewal rule change, or Tourism and Hospitality update that affects access for lower-barrier occupations.
