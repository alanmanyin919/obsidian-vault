## What Changed
- Manitoba posted a new EOI draw on 2026-03-26: Draw #267 issued 14 LAAs, all under the Skilled Worker Stream for candidates directly invited under strategic recruitment initiatives or the temporary public policy route. The split was Employer Services 4, Ethnocultural Communities 1, Francophone Community 8, Regional Communities 0, and TPP 1.
- Manitoba posted a process change on 2026-03-02: MPNP moved applicant inquiries to webforms and said it will no longer respond to inquiries received by email. This is operational rather than an eligibility-rule change, but it affects how applicants follow up.
- Manitoba changed the Temporary Resident Retention Pilot effective 2026-02-04: Hospitality and Food Services was removed as an eligible sector, and several skilled trades occupations were added. This matters for work-linked in-province routes, not for overseas recruitment.

## What Did Not Change
- I did not find a new April 2026 pathway-rule update on Manitoba official pages as of 2026-04-09.
- Skilled Worker Overseas still requires an established Manitoba connection plus at least 60 assessment points. I did not find a posted rule change removing or broadening that requirement.
- The overseas employer-linked route remains the Employer Direct Initiative. It is still described as primarily for recruitment from overseas or outside Canada, with employer-side screening, advertising, wage, and compliance requirements.
- EOI selection still appears tightly controlled. Recent 2026 draws continue to say Skilled Worker candidates were considered only if directly invited under a strategic recruitment initiative or through the temporary public policy route.
- The temporary public policy support-letter page still says the policy expires on 2025-12-31. I did not find a Manitoba-side extension beyond that date.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For an overseas applicant, Manitoba still does not look like an open general-access pathway. Skilled Worker Overseas remains connection-based, and recent draws still favor direct strategic recruitment rather than broad score-threshold invitations.
- For a work-linked path, Manitoba still looks more practical if there is a Manitoba employer connection. The Employer Direct Initiative remains the clearer overseas-facing route, while the TRRP is for people already working in Manitoba.
- The March 26 draw volume was small, which suggests Manitoba is still using narrow targeted selection rather than broad intake for overseas candidates.

## Uncertainties
- The MPNP Monthly Data 2026 page was only updated through February when checked, even though March draws were already posted. That means March totals and any pattern shift are not yet fully reflected in the monthly data page.
- I did not find a fresh Manitoba government news release in April 2026 confirming any additional pathway changes beyond what appears on Manitoba Immigration pages.
- It is possible Manitoba will post another draw or notice after this check; this note only reflects what was visible on official pages as of 2026-04-09T160007Z.
