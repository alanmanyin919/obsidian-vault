## What Changed
- No new Saskatchewan pathway or occupation-policy update was found on 2026-04-09.
- Current official 2026 framework still shows an initial SINP nomination allocation of 4,761, with at least 50 percent reserved for priority sectors and capped shares for accommodation/food services, trucking, and retail trade.
- Current official EOI page still says there are no scheduled International Skilled Worker EOI draws at this time.

## What Did Not Change
- Saskatchewan still uses an EOI-based process for International Skilled Worker Occupation In-Demand and Express Entry.
- Occupation In-Demand still requires TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 work experience tied to the applicant's education and to an occupation considered in demand at the time of application.
- Job-offer pathways still have occupation and employer-side exclusion rules, so not every Saskatchewan job offer is usable for SINP purposes.
