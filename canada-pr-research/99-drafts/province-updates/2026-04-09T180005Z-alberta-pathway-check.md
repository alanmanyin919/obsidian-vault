## What Changed
- No new Alberta AAIP stream eligibility change or intake pause/reopening was posted on the official pages during this check on 2026-04-09.
- The most recent official AAIP update still appears to be the April 7, 2026 Worker Expression of Interest fee change: a $135 fee now applies to Worker EOIs.
- The most recent Alberta immigration-related government news item found was the April 1, 2026 Bill 26 announcement. If passed, it would create an employer registry for temporary foreign worker access and licensing rules for recruiters and immigration consultants, but it is not yet an active AAIP stream-rule change.

## What Did Not Change
- Alberta still uses the Worker EOI invitation system for worker streams.
- Alberta Opportunity Stream remains an employment-linked pathway for people already working full-time in Alberta with an eligible job offer.
- Rural Renewal Stream still requires designated-community endorsement plus a qualifying job offer.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream remains open as a sector-specific employment-linked option for qualifying workers already in Alberta with an eligible tourism or hospitality employer and LMIA-backed employment.
- The Rural Renewal Stream changes announced on November 18, 2025 remain the main recent rule shift already in force for 2026: community endorsement allocation limits, one-year endorsement-letter validity, a TEER-based endorsement model, and a valid-work-permit requirement for in-Canada applicants.
- Alberta’s 2026 processing page still says AAIP priorities are health care, technology, construction, manufacturing, aviation, agriculture, and Rural Renewal communities. That remains more favorable to priority-sector or rural-linked applicants than to generic lower-barrier office/support applicants.

## Relevance To My Situation
- This check does not show a new easier Alberta entry route for lower-barrier roles.
- Alberta still looks most practical only if there is a concrete Alberta job offer tied to an existing stream, especially Rural Renewal or Tourism and Hospitality, rather than a general overseas application strategy.
- For non-priority occupations such as general office/admin or support roles, Alberta still appears relatively competitive and employer-dependent compared with a direct lower-barrier pathway.

## Uncertainties
- AAIP does not publish full upcoming draw parameters, so selection behavior can change without a formal eligibility-rule change.
- The Bill 26 immigration oversight proposal could later affect employers or recruiters using temporary foreign worker channels, but the page says details would come later in regulations and only if the bill is passed and proclaimed.
- This was an official-source check only; no secondary reporting was used to infer timing beyond what Alberta has posted.
