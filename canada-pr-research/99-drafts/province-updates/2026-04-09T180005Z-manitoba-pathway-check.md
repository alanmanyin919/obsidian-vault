## What Changed
- No new Manitoba pathway-rule or draw announcement was found on April 9, 2026 beyond the latest official items already posted in March 2026.
- The latest visible draw remains Expression of Interest Draw #267 dated 2026-03-26. It issued 14 LAAs and continued the same narrow pattern: Skilled Worker candidates were only considered if directly invited under a strategic recruitment initiative or through the now-concluded Temporary Public Policy (TPP).
- The most recent operational change remains the 2026-03-02 contact-process update. This changes how applicants communicate with MPNP, but it does not change pathway eligibility.
- The most recent work-linked program change remains the 2026-02-04 TRRP sector update: hospitality and food services is no longer eligible, while several skilled trades were added.

## What Did Not Change
- Skilled Worker Overseas still requires a Manitoba connection such as Manitoba support, Manitoba experience, or a direct MPNP invitation through a strategic recruitment initiative.
- I did not find any new reopening or broadening of general overseas access. Recent draws still appear tightly limited to strategic-recruitment and TPP-linked candidates rather than broad Skilled Worker Overseas selection.
- I did not find any new Manitoba government notice restoring the TPP route. Officially, new Support Letter applications closed on 2025-12-16, and IRCC acceptance under that policy ended on 2025-12-31.
- I did not find a new March or April 2026 monthly data release on the official MPNP Monthly Data 2026 page. That page still shows January and February totals only.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For an overseas applicant without a Manitoba-specific connection or direct strategic-recruitment invitation, Manitoba still looks hard to access directly through current MPNP draw behavior.
- For work-linked routes, Manitoba remains more realistic if the path starts with Manitoba employment or employer support rather than a pure overseas EOI strategy.
- The February TRRP change matters if the practical plan depends on lower-barrier work in hospitality or food service: that route became weaker after 2026-02-04.
- IT remains listed as an eligible TRRP employer sector, but TRRP is for temporary residents already working in Manitoba, so it does not help a person who is still fully overseas.

## Uncertainties
- I did not find a separate April 2026 Manitoba government news release with new immigration rules, but absence of a found notice is not proof that no internal policy guidance exists.
- The official monthly data page lags behind the individual draw notices, so draw-pattern interpretation for March 2026 is based on draw posts rather than a complete March monthly dataset.
- The draw posts do not clearly separate how many LAAs in each draw went to overseas candidates versus in-Manitoba candidates inside the same strategic-recruitment categories.

## Suggested Next Step
- Treat this as a no-new-change check and keep monitoring Manitoba for either a broader Skilled Worker Overseas draw pattern, a new employer-linked intake change, or any replacement for the expired TPP route.
