## What Changed
- AAIP added a new Worker Expression of Interest fee effective April 7, 2026. Submitting a Worker EOI now costs $135.
- AAIP updated the Worker EOI on March 11, 2026. WEOIs submitted on or after February 25, 2026 must include wage and hours-of-work information for candidates with an Alberta job offer, and AAIP says draws may use these factors. Older WEOIs without that information may be excluded from those draws.
- Alberta's 2026 processing page confirms worker-stream prioritization toward key sectors and communities designated under the Rural Renewal Stream.
- Alberta government immigration news now includes Bill 26, the Immigration Oversight Act, introduced on April 1, 2026. If passed, it would create employer registry requirements for temporary foreign worker hiring and licensing rules for recruiters and immigration consultants. This is not yet an in-force pathway rule, but it could affect employer-side access and compliance later.

## What Did Not Change
- I did not find any new official announcement of an Alberta Opportunity Stream intake pause, reopening, or other fresh stream closure on the pages checked today.
- I did not find any new April 2026 eligibility expansion for lower-barrier worker pathways.
- The main Rural Renewal Stream tightening remains the January 1, 2026 change set already posted by AAIP: community endorsement allocation limits, one-year endorsement validity, a TEER-based endorsement model, and a valid-work-permit requirement for foreign nationals already in Canada.
- Rural Renewal still appears open in practice, with 2026 processing information posted and Rural Renewal draws listed on February 10, February 26, March 13, and March 26, 2026.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream still appears active, with 2026 allocation and processing data posted and a draw listed for February 24, 2026. I did not find a fresh reopening or pause notice for that stream.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For lower-barrier or employment-linked planning, Alberta still looks more viable through employer-linked routes than through broad low-barrier nomination access.
- The clearest practical effect from this check is that Alberta's worker-stream entry is now a bit harder administratively: there is a new EOI fee, and job-offer wage and hours data may now influence selection.
- Rural Renewal is still relevant, but it is tighter than before for candidates outside Alberta or outside Canada because TEER 0 to 3 access is narrower than for temporary workers already in Alberta, and community endorsement limits reduce room for speculative applications.
- Tourism and Hospitality remains one of Alberta's more accessible lower-language pathways on paper, but it still requires current Alberta work experience, an eligible employer, and an eligible occupation, so it is mainly useful after securing in-province employment rather than as an easy direct entry route from abroad.

## Uncertainties
- AAIP does not disclose full current draw parameters, so the exact impact of the new wage and hours fields on invitation chances is unclear.
- The processing page gives current allocations, pool sizes, and draw history, but it does not guarantee continued access or the same draw mix.
- Bill 26 is only introduced, not yet in force. Its eventual regulations and timing are not yet published on the checked pages.
