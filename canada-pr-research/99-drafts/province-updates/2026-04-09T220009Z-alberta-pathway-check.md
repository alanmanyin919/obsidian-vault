## What Changed
- No new Alberta pathway update dated April 9, 2026 was found on the official AAIP pages during this check.
- The latest direct AAIP change remains the new $135 Worker Expression of Interest fee, effective April 7, 2026.
- The latest Worker EOI form change remains the March 11, 2026 update stating that WEOIs submitted on or after February 25, 2026 must include wage and hours information for candidates with an Alberta job offer, and WEOIs missing that information will not be considered in draws that use those factors.
- The latest major lower-barrier / employment-linked stream rule change remains the Rural Renewal Stream update posted November 18, 2025 and effective January 1, 2026, including community endorsement allocation limits, one-year endorsement validity, a TEER-based endorsement model, and a valid-work-permit requirement at application and assessment for foreign nationals already in Canada.
- Alberta’s April 1, 2026 immigration oversight announcement is relevant to employer-linked pathways, but it is not yet an AAIP stream rule change. Bill 26 was introduced on April 1, 2026 and would create employer registry and recruiter / consultant licensing rules if passed and proclaimed.

## What Did Not Change
- Alberta still uses the invitation-only Worker EOI system for worker streams.
- Alberta Opportunity Stream, Tourism and Hospitality Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, and Alberta Express Entry worker pathways remain listed as active.
- I did not find an official notice in this check showing a new intake pause, reopening, or stream closure for Alberta Opportunity Stream, Tourism and Hospitality Stream, or Rural Renewal Stream.
- Alberta’s 2026 processing page still shows active nominations and draws for Alberta Opportunity Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, and Tourism and Hospitality Stream, which suggests these employment-linked routes remain operational.
- Alberta’s 2026 processing priorities still favour sectors such as health care, technology, construction, manufacturing, aviation, agriculture, and designated Rural Renewal communities. This does not look like a new lower-barrier expansion for office-support or general administrative roles.

## Relevance To My Situation
- The clearest practical Alberta change for a worker applicant is that the Worker EOI now costs money and, where there is an Alberta job offer, includes more job-detail screening through wage and hours fields.
- Alberta still appears more concrete for employment-linked routes than for low-barrier non-employer routes. The most realistic pathways remain job-offer-linked options such as Alberta Opportunity Stream, Tourism and Hospitality Stream, and Rural Renewal Stream.
- The Rural Renewal Stream remains relevant if a designated community employer can be secured, but the January 1, 2026 changes make that route more controlled and likely more competitive because of endorsement caps and tighter endorsement rules.
- I did not find a new Alberta change in this check that obviously makes lower-barrier entry easier for someone without a strong Alberta job offer.

- Alberta does not disclose detailed current draw parameters, so it is unclear how strongly wage, hours, and occupation details are already influencing non-public draw selection.
- Bill 26 is only proposed legislation as of April 1, 2026. Its eventual effect on employer-linked pathways depends on whether it passes and how regulations are written.
