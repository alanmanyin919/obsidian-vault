## What Changed
- No new Manitoba pathway-rule update, intake pause/reopening, or April 2026 draw notice was visible on the official pages checked at 2026-04-09T220009Z.
- The latest posted draw still appears to be EOI Draw #267 dated 2026-03-26. It issued 14 LAAs and, like Draw #266 on 2026-03-12, only considered Skilled Worker candidates who had already been directly invited under a strategic recruitment initiative or through the temporary public policy route.
- This means the narrow targeted-draw pattern remains in place. This is an inference from the official draw notices rather than a separate Manitoba statement saying the pattern is permanent.

## What Did Not Change
- Skilled Worker Overseas still requires an established Manitoba connection through Manitoba support, previous Manitoba experience, or a direct MPNP strategic recruitment invitation.
- The EOI page still says profiles stay valid for one year and that Manitoba can limit or exclude occupations from draws.
- Employer Direct Initiative still appears to be the main official overseas or outside-Canada employer-linked route, and it is still described as primarily intended for overseas or outside-Canada recruitment.
- Temporary Resident Retention Pilot still remains for temporary residents already working in Manitoba, and the February 4, 2026 change removing hospitality and food services while expanding some skilled-trade eligibility still appears to be the latest public rule change for that route.
- The MPNP Monthly Data 2026 page still appears to show January and February data only, with no March 2026 monthly update visible during this check.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For an overseas applicant without an existing Manitoba connection, this check still does not show an easier direct-access Manitoba pathway opening up.
- Manitoba still looks more realistic if the plan depends on getting a Manitoba employer relationship, using Employer Direct, or later becoming eligible through a strategic recruitment invitation.
- For work-linked planning, Manitoba remains more relevant through employer-connected routes than through a broad passive EOI approach from overseas.
