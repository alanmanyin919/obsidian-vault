## What Changed
- No meaningful new Saskatchewan pathway or eligibility change was found on this 2026-04-09T220009Z check compared with the earlier 2026-04-09 draft checks.
- The current 2026 rule set remains the operative baseline: the worker fee expansion that took effect on 2026-04-01 is still in force, capped-sector intake windows remain in place, and the official pages still show no scheduled International Skilled Worker EOI draws.
- Saskatchewan still presents technology as a priority lane, and the Tech Talent pathway still includes occupations relevant to this profile's background such as `21230` computer systems developers and programmers, `21232` software developers and programmers, `22221` user support technicians, and `22222` information systems testing technicians.

## What Did Not Change
- Saskatchewan still does not look like an easy no-job-offer route for this profile. The official EOI page still says there are no scheduled EOI draws at this time.
- Saskatchewan still prioritizes sectors rather than broad passive access: priority sectors remain healthcare, agriculture, skilled trades, mining, manufacturing, energy, and technology.
- Retail, trucking, and accommodation/food service remain capped sectors with intake windows and a rule that candidates generally need `6` months or less left on their work permit to use those intake periods.
- The SINP still appears more employer-linked than Manitoba-style worker pathways for non-priority occupations. Job Approval support remains central for Employment Offer and Tech Talent routes.
- The recent Saskatchewan government news items reviewed in this run were compliance and anti-fraud updates, not new pathway openings or broader eligibility relief.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For this profile, Saskatchewan still looks most realistic only if the move is employer-led and tied to a Saskatchewan job offer in a technology-priority occupation. That fits the existing background in software development and enterprise application support better than a generic lower-barrier admin route.
- Saskatchewan looks weaker if the practical first job ends up being generic office support, retail, food service, or another non-priority support role, because the province is still steering nominations toward priority sectors and tighter employer-linked channels.
- The existing valid OWP through `2029-01-27` is still useful because it can make a Saskatchewan employer hire easier in practice, but the official SINP pages reviewed here do not clearly spell out how the Hong Kong public policy OWP is treated across every Saskatchewan Experience subcategory. The safe reading is that a job offer route remains the most concrete Saskatchewan option.
- Weaker spoken English and no strong local network still matter here because Saskatchewan's workable routes remain employer-dependent. That raises the importance of securing a real Saskatchewan employer match before treating this province as a serious primary PR lane.

- The EOI page still shows a no-scheduled-draw message tied to the 2025 federal cut. It is unclear whether Saskatchewan will restart broader EOI draws later in 2026 now that the FAQ lists a higher 2026 allocation.
- This check did not test a live employer case, Job Approval Letter case, or specific Saskatchewan vacancy against SINP approval rules, so practical feasibility for a real target job remains unverified.
