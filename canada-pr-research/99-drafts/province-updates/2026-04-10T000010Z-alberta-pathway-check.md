## What Changed
- AAIP now says that, effective April 7, 2026, a `Worker Expression of Interest (EOI)` fee of `$135` is required to enter the worker-stream pool. This affects worker-stream access generally, including Alberta Opportunity Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, and Tourism and Hospitality Stream.
- AAIP processing guidance updated for 2026 says Alberta will prioritize worker-stream draws and nominations for key sectors such as health care, technology, construction, manufacturing, aviation, agriculture, and communities designated under the Rural Renewal Stream. This is a process-priority change, not a direct stream eligibility expansion.
- Alberta introduced Bill 26, the `Immigration Oversight Act`, on April 1, 2026. If passed, it would add employer registry and recruiter/licensing rules tied to temporary foreign worker oversight. This is relevant to employment-linked pathways, but it is not yet a direct AAIP stream-rule change.

## What Did Not Change
- No new official AAIP update was found showing a fresh intake pause or reopening for Alberta worker streams during this check.
- No new lower-barrier AAIP stream was announced. Alberta still mainly relies on employer-linked or Alberta-based pathways.
- Alberta Opportunity Stream still requires the applicant to already be living and working in Alberta with a valid work permit and an eligible Alberta job offer.
- Tourism and Hospitality Stream still requires full-time Alberta work in the sector, an eligible occupation, an approved employer, and a positive LMIA.
- The most recent major stream-rule change on the AAIP updates page remains the Rural Renewal Stream changes announced November 18, 2025 and effective January 1, 2026.

## Relevance To My Situation
- Alberta still does not look like an easy direct route from outside Canada for a lower-barrier applicant. The main worker options remain strongly tied to already having Alberta work authorization, an Alberta employer, or a designated rural community endorsement.
- The Rural Renewal Stream remains the most clearly employment-linked Alberta route if a real employer and designated community can be secured, but the 2026 priorities suggest Alberta is being selective and not simply broadening access.
- The new `$135` Worker EOI fee raises the cost of trying Alberta pathways, even before the full worker-stream application fee.

## Uncertainties
- AAIP does not publish full draw-selection parameters, and it explicitly says EOI score is not the only selection factor.
- The April 7, 2026 Worker EOI fee appears on the official worker-stream application page, but I did not find a separate AAIP updates entry announcing that fee change.
- Bill 26 is proposed legislation as of April 1, 2026. Its practical impact on employers and recruiters depends on whether it passes and when regulations are implemented.

## Suggested Next Step
- Keep Alberta in the watchlist, but treat it as a selective employer-linked option rather than a low-barrier fallback. On the next Alberta check, focus on whether AAIP posts any new draw behavior, stream-specific reopening/pause notice, or Rural Renewal/Tourism and Hospitality rule easing.
