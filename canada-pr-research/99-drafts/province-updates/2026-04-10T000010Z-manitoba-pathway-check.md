## What Changed
- A new MPNP EOI draw was posted for April 9, 2026: Draw #268 issued 32 Letters of Advice to Apply under strategic recruitment only.
- Draw #268 breakdown: Employer Services 10, Ethnocultural Communities 5, Francophone Community 7, Regional Communities 5, and Temporary Public Policy to Facilitate Work Permits for Prospective Provincial Nominee Program Candidates (TPP) 5.
- The latest draw continues the recent pattern from March 12, 2026, March 26, 2026, and February 2026 draws: Manitoba is selecting Skilled Worker in Manitoba and Skilled Worker Overseas candidates who were directly invited under strategic initiatives, rather than showing a broad open overseas draw.
- No newer pathway-rule change was posted after the February 5, 2026 Temporary Resident Retention Pilot update. That update remains the latest clear rule change affecting work-linked routes: hospitality and food services were removed from TRRP eligibility after February 4, 2026, while several skilled trades occupations were added.

## What Did Not Change
- Skilled Worker Overseas still requires an established Manitoba connection through Manitoba Support, Manitoba Experience, or a direct MPNP invitation under a Strategic Recruitment Initiative.
- Employer-linked Manitoba routes still look stronger than pure overseas access. Employer Services and TRRP remain active official channels for work-linked cases.
- The Temporary Public Policy support-letter window remains closed to new Manitoba applications after December 15, 2025, with IRCC acceptance under that temporary policy ending December 31, 2025.
- Employer Services still notes longer-than-target processing because of high TRRP application volume.
- Manitoba monthly 2026 data still shows modest skilled-worker LAA volumes so far this year: 102 in January and 101 in February.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For an overseas applicant without a strong Manitoba tie, Manitoba still does not look like an easy direct-access pathway right now. The official draw pattern continues to favor candidates pulled through strategic recruitment channels rather than a broad general overseas lane.
- For work-linked routes, Manitoba still appears more realistic if the path is employer-backed or already Manitoba-based. That is stronger for people with Manitoba employment, an employer willing to engage with Employer Services, or an eligible temporary resident profile already in the province.
- The February 2026 TRRP change makes hospitality weaker as a Manitoba retention route, while certain skilled trades became more favored. That does not help a general office-support or overseas-first profile much unless there is a direct employer strategy.
