## What Changed
- No new Saskatchewan pathway change was found on official pages for this April 10, 2026 check.
- The most recent material still in force is that, effective 2026-04-01, SINP extended its $500 application fee and $250 second review fee to all worker applicants in all categories.
- Current 2026 SINP rules still show 4,761 initial nominations, with at least 50 percent reserved for priority sectors and 25 percent capped across accommodation/food services, trucking, and retail.
- Current 2026 rules still show capped-sector intake windows tied to candidates whose work permits expire within six months.

## What Did Not Change
- Saskatchewan still appears more restrictive for overseas applicants outside health care, agriculture, and skilled trades than it was before the 2025 restructuring.
- Entrepreneur, International Graduate Entrepreneur, and Farm Owner/Operator categories remain closed.
- Occupations In-Demand and Saskatchewan Express Entry still do not function as broad no-job-offer options in the way they once did; the current system remains strongly job-offer and employer-approval driven.
- The Innovation and Tech Talent Pathway remains active and still appears to include support-relevant tech occupations such as user support technicians and information systems testing technicians.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For a profile centered on admin/support or general office work, Saskatchewan still looks weak because the province is prioritizing health care, agriculture, skilled trades, and other designated priority sectors.
- For a profile that can credibly target tech support or testing roles, Saskatchewan remains more feasible through employer-linked routes such as Tech Talent or Employment Offer, but only with a Saskatchewan job offer and employer participation in the SINP process.
- For an overseas applicant without a Saskatchewan employer, this check does not show any reopening of a broad low-barrier pathway.
- The new all-category worker fee is a practical negative change because it raises the cost of trying Saskatchewan once an employer-backed pathway becomes available.

## Uncertainties
- The official pages do not clearly state on one single page whether any additional NOC codes were added to Tech Talent on 2026-01-01 beyond the currently listed occupations; the pathway page shows the present eligible list, which should be treated as the operative rule.
- This draft assumes the target profile is still closer to support, service-desk, application-support, QA, or similar lower-barrier work than to regulated health or skilled-trades occupations.
- Saskatchewan may make mid-year allocation adjustments in 2026, but no new change notice was found during this check.

## Suggested Next Step
- Keep Saskatchewan in the draft shortlist only as a job-offer-dependent option, and treat it as materially more realistic for tech-support/testing lanes than for admin/support lanes unless a new official pathway change appears.
