## What Changed
- Alberta added a new `Worker Expression of Interest` fee of `$135`, effective `2026-04-07`. This matters because Alberta worker streams now depend on the WEOI system before invitation, including employment-linked options such as Alberta Opportunity Stream, Rural Renewal Stream, and Tourism and Hospitality Stream.
- Alberta updated the WEOI form on `2026-03-11` to require wage and hours information for candidates with an Alberta job offer. Alberta says WEOIs submitted without that information will not be considered in draws that use those factors.
- The most important employment-linked rule change still in force is the `Rural Renewal Stream` update effective `2026-01-01`: community endorsement allocation limits, one-year validity for endorsement letters, a TEER-based endorsement model, and a valid-work-permit requirement for foreign nationals already in Canada at application and assessment.
- Alberta’s `2026` processing page says the province is prioritizing worker-stream draws and nominations for key sectors and designated rural communities. That suggests general lower-barrier access is still constrained by targeted selection rather than broad reopening.

## What Did Not Change
- I did not find a new Alberta government notice showing a fresh pause, reopening, or eligibility rewrite for the `Alberta Opportunity Stream`.
- I did not find a new official notice showing a fresh pause, reopening, or eligibility rewrite for the `Tourism and Hospitality Stream`.
- I did not find a new official notice showing a new post-January-1-2026 eligibility rewrite for the `Rural Renewal Stream`; the January 2026 rules still appear to be the active baseline.
- Alberta still shows active worker-stream processing and draw activity in 2026 rather than a full shutdown of these pathways.

## Relevance To My Situation
- Alberta still looks more realistic if I first secure real Alberta employment, because the main lower-barrier pathways remain work-linked rather than open to overseas applicants without a job.
- The `Tourism and Hospitality Stream` remains one of the clearest lower-barrier options on paper, but it is tightly limited: approved employer, eligible occupation, valid work permit, and at least 6 months with the same employer.
- The `Rural Renewal Stream` is still potentially useful for employment-linked entry, but the tighter endorsement controls and targeted rural-community model make it less open-ended than older summaries might suggest.
- The new WEOI fee and added wage/hours fields increase friction slightly and make job quality details more relevant in selection.

## Uncertainties
- Alberta does not publish full draw-selection logic, so it is unclear how much wage level, hours, occupation, and employer type now influence WEOI invitations across worker streams.
- The April 1, 2026 immigration oversight bill is official government news, but it is not yet a direct AAIP pathway eligibility change. Its practical effect depends on whether it passes and how regulations are written later.
- I did not verify community-by-community Rural Renewal endorsement practices in this check; those can change even when province-wide rules do not.
