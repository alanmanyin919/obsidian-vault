## What Changed
- A new official draw was posted on April 9, 2026: Expression of Interest Draw #268.
- Draw #268 issued 32 Letters of Advice to Apply, including 10 through Employer Services, 7 through the Francophone community, 5 through regional communities, 5 through ethnocultural communities, and 5 through the temporary public policy for prospective provincial nominee candidates.
- The latest draw continues the recent pattern where Skilled Worker invitations are limited to candidates directly invited under strategic recruitment initiatives or the temporary public policy route, rather than broad general-pool invitations.

## What Did Not Change
- I did not find a newly published rewrite of the main Skilled Worker in Manitoba or Skilled Worker Overseas pathway structure during this check.
- The Skilled Worker Stream still officially shows two main pathways: Skilled Worker in Manitoba and Skilled Worker Overseas.
- The overseas route still appears tied in practice to Manitoba connection and direct-invitation mechanisms, with recent draws continuing to target strategic recruitment and temporary public policy candidates.
- Work-linked Manitoba routes remain active through employer-linked channels such as Employer Direct Initiative and the Temporary Resident Retention Pilot.
- No new official April 2026 notice was found changing TRRP eligibility beyond the February 5, 2026 update that removed hospitality and food services and added several skilled trades.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For an overseas applicant, the official draw pattern still looks narrow and invitation-driven rather than open and broad. That makes Manitoba harder to treat as a simple direct-apply province without a Manitoba-linked recruitment path.
- For work-linked routes, Manitoba still looks more realistic when tied to an employer-supported route or a Manitoba-based temporary resident path rather than a standalone overseas EOI.
- If the target profile is office support, application support, or similar non-trade roles, the February TRRP trade expansion does not appear to improve access directly. Employer-linked recruitment remains more relevant than the trade-focused TRRP change.

## Uncertainties
- Manitoba monthly data is only posted through February 2026 on the official data page, so March and April totals are not yet reflected there.
- This check confirms what is currently published on official pages as of April 9, 2026, but it does not prove whether internal selection practice has shifted beyond what the posted draw notices show.
- I did not find a separate Manitoba government news release adding a new pathway rule after the April 9 draw; if one exists but is not yet indexed or published, this check would miss it.

## Suggested Next Step
- Keep treating Manitoba as a province where employer-linked or strategic-invitation routes matter more than a generic overseas EOI, and recheck the next official draw or any new Manitoba immigration notice for a broader rule change.
