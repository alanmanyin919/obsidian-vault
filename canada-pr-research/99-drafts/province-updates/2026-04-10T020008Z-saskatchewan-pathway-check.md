## What Changed
- Current official SINP pages confirm the 2026 framework is active. Saskatchewan's initial 2026 nomination allocation is 4,761, and unlike 2025 there is no federal rule requiring a fixed share of nominees to already be temporary residents in Canada.
- Saskatchewan is still operating with sector prioritization for 2026: at least 50 per cent of nominations are reserved for priority sectors including health care, agriculture, skilled trades, mining, manufacturing, energy, and technology.
- Capped-sector rules are active for 2026. Accommodation and food services, trucking, and retail trade are limited to 25 per cent of the annual allocation combined, and employers in those sectors can only submit Job Approval Forms during six 2026 intake windows for workers with six months or less left on their work permit.
- Effective April 1, 2026, the SINP's $500 application fee and $250 second-review fee now apply to all worker applicants in all categories.
- Saskatchewan's Innovation and Tech Talent Pathway officially expanded its eligible occupations as of January 1, 2026. The current list includes NOCs such as 22220 `Computer network and web technicians`, 22221 `User support technicians`, and 22222 `Information systems testing technicians`, which is more relevant to practical IT-support or QA-adjacent profiles than a narrow software-developer-only lane.
- Current official guidance continues to restrict PGWP and some open-work-permit routes. PGWP holders are now limited to the Student, Health Talent, Agriculture Talent, Innovation and Tech Talent, and International Skilled Worker: Employment Offer pathways. PGWP holders who studied outside Saskatchewan are no longer eligible for Saskatchewan Experience pathways.

## What Did Not Change
- I did not find a newer Saskatchewan government news release after the existing 2025 SINP restructuring that announced the major pathway reset, including overseas prioritization for health, agriculture, and skilled trades and the return of no-job-offer applications in Saskatchewan Express Entry and Occupations In-Demand.
- Entrepreneur, International Graduate Entrepreneur, and Farm Owner/Operator categories still appear closed.
- International Skilled Worker: Employment Offer remains the main general Saskatchewan route for a person with a Saskatchewan job offer in a high-skilled TEER 0 to 3 occupation, subject to the points grid and employer Job Approval Letter requirements.
- Technology remains a priority sector in 2026, and the tech pathway remains available, but it is still occupation-specific rather than a broad open pathway.

## Relevance To My Situation
- For a profile relying on lower-barrier office, admin, customer support, or general non-trade work, Saskatchewan still looks harder than provinces where broader employer-linked or lower-skill routes are more open. The 2026 priority design clearly favors health, agriculture, trades, and selected tech-aligned roles.
- If the realistic occupation lane is IT support, application support, user support, network/web technician work, or QA-style testing work with a Saskatchewan employer, Saskatchewan is more usable than before through either International Skilled Worker: Employment Offer or the Innovation and Tech Talent Pathway. That is still much more realistic with a concrete job offer than through speculative no-job-offer options.
- If the likely job is in retail, food service, or trucking, feasibility is weaker because those sectors are capped and now tied to narrow intake windows plus the six-month work-permit timing rule.
- If the plan depends on studying outside Saskatchewan and later using a Saskatchewan Experience route on a PGWP, current official rules are less favorable than before.
