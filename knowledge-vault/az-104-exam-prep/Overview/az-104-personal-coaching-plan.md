# AZ-104 Personal Coaching Plan

## Exam Window
- Today: March 17, 2026
- Exam date: April 9, 2026
- Time remaining: 23 calendar days including exam day

## Constraint
You work in the office from `10:00 AM` to `7:00 PM`, so this plan is built for:
- short weekday study windows
- longer weekend blocks
- high-yield practice over broad but shallow reading

## Coaching Rules
1. Do not "study everything." Study the exam's operational patterns.
2. Every session must produce output:
   a summary, a checklist update, a lab result, or 5-10 recall questions.
3. Prefer active recall and hands-on work over rereading notes.
4. Track weak areas aggressively. Weak topics decide the next session.
5. Protect consistency. `75 minutes` daily is better than occasional long sessions.

## Weekly Structure
### Phase 1: Build core admin understanding
- March 17 to March 22
- Focus:
  Identity, governance, storage
- Outcome:
  you should be able to explain scope, RBAC, storage choices, and common admin actions without looking at notes

### Phase 2: Compute and networking
- March 23 to March 29
- Focus:
  VMs, disks, availability, VNets, NSGs, routing, DNS, connectivity
- Outcome:
  you should be able to reason through deployment and troubleshooting scenarios

### Phase 3: Monitoring, backup, and consolidation
- March 30 to April 5
- Focus:
  Monitor, alerts, Log Analytics, backup, recovery, mixed-topic review
- Outcome:
  you should be able to connect services together operationally

### Phase 4: Final exam sprint
- April 6 to April 8
- Focus:
  weak-area repair, timed recall, high-frequency mistakes, light review
- Outcome:
  no new heavy topics; only tighten recall and decision-making

### Exam day
- April 9
- Focus:
  calm review only, no cramming

## Default Weekday Routine
### Lunch or commute review: 15-20 minutes
- Read one small section
- Write 3-5 recall prompts in your note
- Review one prior weak topic

### Evening deep work: 8:15 PM to 9:30 PM
- 10 minutes:
  review yesterday's recall prompts
- 40 minutes:
  one focused concept or one practical task
- 20 minutes:
  write summary from memory
- 15 minutes:
  update checklist and set tomorrow's target

## Default Weekend Routine
### Block 1: 90 minutes
- core concept study
- hands-on task or portal walkthrough

### Block 2: 90 minutes
- second topic
- troubleshooting or recall quiz

### Block 3: 45 minutes
- review errors
- update notes
- decide next week's weak areas

## What Good Progress Looks Like
- You can explain RBAC scope inheritance clearly
- You can choose storage redundancy with a reason
- You can describe when a VM issue is NSG, DNS, route, or disk related
- You can explain what Monitor, logs, alerts, and backup each do
- You can answer scenario questions by elimination, not guessing

## If You Miss A Day
- Do not try to "catch up" by doubling everything
- Move the missed topic into the next available weekday
- Keep the weekend review block for backlog cleanup
