# Internet Research Summary

## Research Goal
Identify what kinds of website features are most defensible for helping people with stress, pressure, and mental health strain, and identify what should be avoided.

## Main Findings
### 1. Digital mental health can help, but structure matters
Broad evidence supports digital interventions for stress, anxiety, and depression when they are:
- structured
- skills-based
- based on known methods such as CBT or mindfulness
- clear about limits

Inference:
- a guided program is safer and more useful than a free-form chatbot pretending to be a therapist

### 2. Stress-management content should be practical, short, and repeatable
Official guidance from WHO, NHS, NIMH, and CDC consistently emphasizes:
- breathing or grounding
- physical activity
- sleep hygiene
- social connection
- journaling or emotional processing
- reaching out for support when symptoms persist or worsen

Inference:
- the site should focus on small actions users can do in 3 to 10 minutes, not long lectures

### 3. Workplace or high-pressure stress needs a different frame from general wellness
People in high-pressure environments often need:
- fast decompression
- help naming what kind of pressure they are under
- a way to separate overload, anxiety, burnout, isolation, and crisis
- help deciding the next safe step

Inference:
- the site should route users by state, not just show a generic homepage

### 4. Crisis handling must be explicit
If the website touches mental illness or severe distress, it needs:
- crisis language
- emergency disclaimers
- visible escalation to local hotlines, emergency services, or trusted contacts
- guidance to seek professional help when symptoms are severe, persistent, or worsening

Inference:
- safety routing is part of the product, not a footer afterthought

### 5. Peer support is useful only if moderated
Online peer support can reduce isolation, but unmoderated spaces can create:
- misinformation
- harmful advice
- emotional contagion
- triggering content

Inference:
- do not launch with an open community forum unless moderation is part of the design

## Product Features With Strongest Support
- daily stress check-in
- guided breathing or grounding exercises
- short mindfulness audio or text flows
- structured journaling prompts
- CBT-style thought reframing
- burnout and overload checklists
- support directory and escalation page
- personal coping plan

## Features To Avoid Early
- AI therapist persona
- diagnosis claims
- medication advice
- suicide-risk assessment without professional design and escalation
- anonymous unmoderated community forum
- addictive engagement loops
