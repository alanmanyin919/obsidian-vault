# Website Concept

## Working Idea
Build a website for people in high-pressure environments that helps them:
- decompress quickly
- understand what kind of stress they are facing
- choose a practical next step
- build healthier coping habits
- find human support when self-help is not enough

## Best Product Frame
Name-level concept:
- pressure relief companion
- guided stress reset
- high-pressure recovery toolkit

Mission:
- help users move from overload to clarity through short, structured, evidence-based support flows

## Core User Scenarios
### Scenario 1: Immediate overload
User state:
- racing thoughts
- too many tasks
- cannot think clearly

Best website response:
- 2-minute grounding
- breathing visual
- reduce-noise screen with one next step only

### Scenario 2: Ongoing pressure and burnout signs
User state:
- exhausted
- emotionally flat
- dread about work
- poor sleep

Best website response:
- burnout check-in
- journaling prompt
- workload reflection tool
- plan for recovery routines and boundaries

### Scenario 3: Anxiety spirals
User state:
- catastrophizing
- fear of failure
- mental looping

Best website response:
- CBT-style thought unpacking
- evidence-for and evidence-against prompt
- action-versus-rumination split

### Scenario 4: Deeper distress
User state:
- hopelessness
- inability to function
- possible safety concerns

Best website response:
- stop normal flow
- show immediate support options
- encourage contact with local crisis or professional help

## Recommended Feature Set
### A. State-Based Entry
Homepage question:
- What feels closest right now?

Options:
- I am overwhelmed
- I am anxious
- I feel burned out
- I feel low or numb
- I need urgent support

### B. Guided Relief Flows
Short tools:
- breathing
- grounding
- muscle relaxation
- short compassion prompt
- guided mental reset

### C. Structured Self-Help Tools
- thought reframing
- stress trigger journal
- pressure map
- overload triage
- coping plan builder

### D. Recovery Layer
- sleep reset guidance
- movement prompts
- reconnect-with-someone prompts
- after-work decompression ritual builder

### E. Safety Layer
- crisis page
- trusted contact plan
- region-specific support links where possible
- strong disclaimer that the site is not emergency care

## Important Design Principles
- calm but not childish
- no guilt-based streaks
- low stimulation
- text-first with optional audio
- mobile-first
- privacy-respecting
- minimal account requirements for early use

## What Not To Build First
- social feed
- gamified mood streak pressure
- infinite AI chat
- diagnosis dashboard
- “productivity therapy” framing
