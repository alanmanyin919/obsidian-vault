# Canada PR Research Dashboard

## Purpose
This workspace is the main decision-support area for Canada permanent residence planning, immigration research, and relocation evaluation. It is designed to support practical research, structured comparison, and low-friction review when energy is limited.

## Current Mode
- Project status: Restarting from Phase 1 as of 2026-04-04
- Interpretation:
  - Existing province, pathway, job, and resume notes remain useful as draft research
  - They should not be treated as final direction until Phase 1 and Phase 2 are re-confirmed

## Current Situation Summary
- Hong Kong user, age 32
- Valid Canada Open Work Permit (OWP), issued on 2026-01-28 and expiring on 2029-01-27
- Previously visited Canada from 2026-01-28 to 2026-02-04
- Progress has been limited since returning to Hong Kong
- Total assets/savings may be around HKD 600,000, but the actual budget currently available for a Canada plan is HKD 100,000
- No strong local network in Canada
- English communication is a constraint, especially speaking and general workplace communication
- Mental health is a serious planning factor and must be treated as part of the decision, not as a side issue

See [[canada-pr-research/01-core/01-personal-profile]] for the factual baseline.

## Key Decisions To Make
- Should I try Canada at all?
- If yes, what kind of move makes sense: trial move, job-first move, or more committed relocation?
- Which province or region is realistic for my profile?
- Which job paths are realistic enough to support survival and later PR strategy?
- How should I use the remaining OWP window?

See [[canada-pr-research/01-core/02-should-i-try-canada]], [[canada-pr-research/01-core/04-province-comparison]], and [[canada-pr-research/05-planning/01-owp-strategy]].

## My Constraints
- Limited Canada network
- Weaker English for spoken/workplace communication
- Need for low-chaos planning
- Mental health risk under uncertainty and isolation
- Need to protect limited deployable capital rather than drift into expensive trial-and-error

## Risk Reminders
- Do not treat OWP possession as proof that a Canada move is automatically wise
- Do not confuse immigration possibility with job market reality
- Do not ignore mental health deterioration during planning or relocation
- Do not let unclear timelines consume savings without a stop-loss rule
- Do not build plans that depend on optimism, luck, or instant adaptation
