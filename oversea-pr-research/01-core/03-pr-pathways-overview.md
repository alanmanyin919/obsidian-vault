# PR Pathways Overview

## Purpose
This note tracks the main Canada PR pathways worth researching for this profile. It is not a legal conclusion and should not contain invented eligibility details.

## Research Status
- Last updated: 2026-04-04
- Current working assumption:
  - Valid OWP exists, so the core question is not whether work authorization exists
  - The core question is which pathway stack gives the highest PR probability within the OWP window ending on 2029-01-27
- Working principle:
  - Do not treat one pathway as the whole plan
  - Build overlapping options wherever the rules allow it

## Working Rule
- Record only verified facts or clearly mark items as `To Research`
- Use the detailed pathway notes for ongoing updates

## Pathways To Track

### CEC
- Why it matters:
  - It is the cleanest OWP-to-PR pathway if qualifying Canadian work experience is obtained
- Key questions:
  - What job types would realistically build toward TEER 0 to 3 qualifying work?
  - What language result would be needed once qualifying work is obtained?
- Detailed note: [[canada-pr-research/03-pathways/CEC]]

### Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP)
- Why it matters:
  - Provincial routes may be more realistic than relying only on federal competition
  - Express Entry-aligned nomination can add 600 CRS points, so a nomination can sharply improve invitation odds
- Key questions:
  - Which provinces are more practical for this profile?
  - Which streams need local work, employer support, or region-specific ties?
- Detailed note: [[canada-pr-research/03-pathways/PNP]]

### Alberta Advantage Immigration Program (AAIP)
- Why it matters:
  - Alberta appears worth tracking due to lower cost than Toronto/Vancouver and multiple pathways
- Key questions:
  - Which AAIP streams fit OWP holders or workers with realistic first jobs?
- Detailed note: [[canada-pr-research/03-pathways/AAIP]]

### Alberta Opportunity Stream
- Why it matters:
  - May be relevant if working in Alberta under eligible conditions
- Key questions:
  - Does the exact OWP type fall under Alberta's accepted work permit categories?
  - Can a real Alberta full-time job offer in an eligible occupation be secured?
  - Is Alberta a better first move than Atlantic or rural employer-driven routes?
- Detailed note: [[canada-pr-research/03-pathways/alberta-opportunity-stream]]

### Alberta Express Entry Stream
- Why it matters:
  - May matter if an Express Entry profile becomes active later
  - Could be stronger than AOS if Alberta priority factors align
- Key questions:
  - Would occupation, sector, or Alberta connection create a realistic selection chance?
  - Is this only relevant after language results and ECA are in place?
- Detailed note: [[canada-pr-research/03-pathways/alberta-express-entry-stream]]

### Rural Renewal Stream
- Why it matters:
  - Smaller communities may reduce competition and improve employer-linked PR alignment
  - This may be a more practical Alberta route than AOS if a designated community and employer fit can be found
- Key questions:
  - Which designated communities are realistic for target jobs?
  - How much employer dependency and adaptation risk would this create?
- Detailed note: [[canada-pr-research/03-pathways/rural-renewal-stream]]

### Manitoba-Related Routes
- Why it matters:
  - Manitoba is often discussed as a practical province for settlement and immigration
- To Research:
  - Worker streams
  - Regional demand
  - Network/employer dependency
- Detailed note: [[canada-pr-research/03-pathways/manitoba-pathways]]

### Saskatchewan-Related Routes
- Why it matters:
  - Saskatchewan may offer lower cost and different competition dynamics
- To Research:
  - Worker pathways
  - In-demand occupation fit
  - Settlement realism
- Detailed note: [[canada-pr-research/03-pathways/saskatchewan-pathways]]

### Atlantic Pathways
- Why it matters:
  - Atlantic pathways remain one of the strongest alternatives to big-city competition
  - AIP can work as an employer-driven backup to CEC and PNP
- Key questions:
  - Can a designated employer be found in a realistic job type?
  - Does Atlantic job reality offset the lower-chaos immigration structure?
- Detailed note: [[canada-pr-research/03-pathways/atlantic-pathways]]

### Francophone And Category-Based Express Entry Routes
- Why it matters:
  - Current category-based selection materially affects PR odds
  - French can create an additional path outside Quebec and may combine well with regional programs
- Key questions:
  - Is French improvement realistic enough to matter during the OWP window?
  - Do target job types overlap with current category-based priority occupations?
- Detailed note:
  - Track through [[canada-pr-research/03-pathways/pr-pathways-analysis]]

### Other Realistic Pathways Worth Tracking
- Any route directly linked to:
  - Actual job market fit
  - Manageable language burden
  - Lower first-year survival pressure
- Add new notes only when there is a concrete reason
