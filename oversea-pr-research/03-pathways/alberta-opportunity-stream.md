# Alberta Opportunity Stream

## Purpose
Detailed research note for this Alberta pathway.

## Why It Matters
- May be relevant if working in Alberta under eligible conditions
- This stream is worth tracking because Alberta remains a plausible lower-chaos comparison province
- It should not be treated as automatically open to every OWP holder

## Research Status
- Last updated: 2026-04-04
- Source standard:
  - Recheck against the official Alberta AOS page and the AOS eligibility page before acting

## Eligibility Summary
- Must be living and working in Alberta at the time of application
- Must have a full-time job in Alberta from an Alberta employer
- Must be working in an eligible occupation
- Must hold valid temporary resident status and a valid work permit of an accepted type
- Language, education, work experience, and income threshold rules also apply, depending on stream details and occupation

## What Matters For This Profile
- AOS is not a first-step arrival plan by itself
- It becomes relevant only after a real Alberta landing strategy is already working
- The key bottleneck is not only immigration paperwork
  - It is securing Alberta employment in an eligible occupation under the right permit conditions

## OWP Relevance
- A generic statement such as "I have an OWP" is not enough to assume AOS eligibility
- Alberta specifies accepted work permit categories, including some LMIA-based permits, some LMIA-exempt permits, International Experience Canada, Mobilite Francophone, certain public-policy open work permits, and some PGWP-linked cases
- This means the exact permit basis matters
- If the current OWP falls under an accepted category, AOS may become a real option
- If it does not, AOS should not be relied on as the main Alberta plan

## Practical Interpretation
- AOS can be useful as a conditional Alberta backup if all of the following become true:
  - Alberta is chosen as the landing province
  - A full-time Alberta job offer is secured
  - The occupation is eligible
  - The exact work permit type is accepted by Alberta
- If those conditions are not met, Alberta Express Entry Stream or Rural Renewal may be more practical Alberta-linked routes to track

## Current Judgment
- Relevant, but conditional
- Do not treat as the default best Alberta pathway
- Do not treat as proof that Alberta is the best province
- Most useful if a real Alberta job path appears and permit-type fit is confirmed

## Evidence Notes
- Official Alberta pages reviewed on 2026-04-04 indicate that AOS requires active Alberta work, a full-time Alberta job offer, eligible occupation fit, and an accepted work permit basis
- The stream no longer reads like a generic "arrive on OWP and apply later" shortcut
- This note should be updated again only after confirming the exact OWP category against the accepted list

## Questions For This Profile
- Would likely target jobs qualify?
- Is this compatible with a cautious first-year survival strategy?
- Does the exact current OWP type appear on Alberta's accepted list?
- Is Alberta employment realistically easier to secure than Atlantic or rural designated-employer routes?
