# OWP Strategy

## Purpose
This note tracks how to use the Open Work Permit window rationally instead of wasting it through delay or unclear planning.

## Current Known Facts
- Valid Canada Open Work Permit is already held
- Canada visit took place from 2026-01-28 to 2026-02-04
- OWP was issued on 2026-01-28
- OWP is valid until 2029-01-27
- As of 2026-03-26, there are 1038 days left until the permit expiry date
- This is a hard countdown, not a soft planning horizon
- If still in Canada on or after 2029-01-28 without another valid status, remaining there would not be justified by this OWP

## Main Questions
- What is the latest rational time to attempt a move?
- Is a trial move better than a full commitment move?
- Should job search begin before departure, after arrival, or both?

## Risks Of Wasting The OWP Window
- Delaying until there is too little time to use it properly
- Entering Canada without preparation just to “use” the permit
- Spending money on a trial that does not answer the real decision
- Confusing legal permission to work with realistic employability
- Forgetting that every month of delay reduces the remaining legal stay window under the current permit

## Strategy Options
### Trial Move
- Purpose:
  - Test settlement reality, job search difficulty, and personal adaptation
- Benefits:
  - Better information
- Risks:
  - Expensive if unstructured
  - May produce weak results if too short or too chaotic

### Full Commitment Move
- Purpose:
  - Relocate with stronger intention to stay and build a path
- Benefits:
  - More serious job search and adaptation effort
- Risks:
  - Higher stress and cost if the plan is weak

### Job Search Before Departure
- Benefits:
  - Lower immediate cost
  - Better information before moving
- Risks:
  - Some employers may prefer candidates already in Canada

### Job Search After Arrival
- Benefits:
  - Stronger local availability signal
- Risks:
  - Burn rate begins immediately
  - Pressure may worsen decision quality

## Working Timeline
- OWP expiry date: 2029-01-27
- Earliest target action date: 2026-04-01 or later, only after defining a budget cap and trial purpose
- Latest rational departure date: should be set earlier than permit expiry because the permit end date is also the end of this current stay authorization window
- Decision review checkpoint: first formal review should happen before 2026-06-30

## Decision Windows
### Window A: Low-pressure preparation phase
- Date range: 2026-03-26 to 2026-12-31
- Best use:
  - Clarify budget, language position, target job paths, and province shortlist
  - Test pre-arrival job search effort without immediate burn
- Interpretation:
  - This is still the safest period for research and setup, but it should not be treated as free time because the permit clock is already running

### Window B: Strong action phase
- Date range: 2027-01-01 to 2027-12-31
- Best use:
  - Make a trial-move or job-first decision if enough evidence has been gathered
  - Use the OWP while there is still meaningful time left for adjustment after arrival
- Interpretation:
  - This is likely the strongest decision window if the plan becomes viable because enough time may still remain to work, adapt, and pivot

### Window C: Late but still usable phase
- Date range: 2028-01-01 to 2028-06-30
- Best use:
  - Proceed only if the plan is already concrete and financially controlled
  - Avoid exploratory or chaotic relocation
- Interpretation:
  - This window may still be usable, but it is already a late-stage move because the remaining legal stay time is shrinking fast

### Window D: High-risk late phase
- Date range: 2028-07-01 to 2029-01-27
- Best use:
  - Only for a very specific and pre-validated move
- Interpretation:
  - A first-time exploratory move in this window is likely poor risk management because the remaining permit time may be too short to justify relocation cost

## Latest Rational Move Framework
- A move before 2027-12-31 can still be treated as a serious strategic attempt if budget, target role, and stop-loss rules are in place
- A move between 2028-01-01 and 2028-06-30 should be treated as conditional, not default
- A first move after 2028-06-30 should generally be presumed irrational unless there is a clear job-led reason
- A first exploratory move after 2028-12-31 should generally be treated as too late
- The permit expiry date should be read as a latest lawful end point for this status, not as a planning cushion

## Go / Delay / Stop Heuristic
### Go
- Budget cap is defined using the HKD 200,000 deployable-budget assumption
- Trial purpose is explicit
- A target province and 2-3 target job paths are identified
- The move can happen while enough OWP time remains for course correction

### Delay
- OWP remains valid for a meaningful period, but language, employability, or budget assumptions are still too weak
- Research is producing real clarity rather than avoidance

### Stop
- The plan remains vague past 2028-06-30
- The move depends on hope rather than evidence
- The financial downside becomes unacceptable under the HKD 200,000 assumption
- Mental health risk becomes too high for an uncertain relocation attempt
