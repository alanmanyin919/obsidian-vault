## What Changed
- This run resolved the main UK rules inconsistency more cleanly than the prior note. The strongest current official source is the Home Office caseworker guidance updated on 2026-04-08, which explicitly says eligibility was expanded to BN(O)s' adult children born on or after 1979-07-01.
- The same updated guidance says the adult child of a BN(O) status holder should apply independently of their BN(O) parent. That makes this route more concrete than a same-household-only reading would suggest for a direct BN(O) parent-child case.
- The official guidance also confirms the route allows work and study, can be granted for 30 months or 5 years, and leads to settlement after 5 years of continuous residence.
- Practical cost is now clearer from the current GOV.UK fee page. For one adult, the visa fee is GBP 206 for 30 months or GBP 285 for 5 years, plus the immigration health surcharge of GBP 2,587.50 for 30 months or GBP 5,175 for 5 years.
- The key proof burden is now better defined: identity, proof of BN(O) status or eligible relationship, proof of ordinary residence where relevant, 6 months of maintenance and accommodation, and a tuberculosis certificate if applying from a listed country.
