## What Did Not Change
- The January 21, 2025 restriction on family-member OWPs still stands. IRCC still limits this measure mainly to spouses or common-law partners of certain workers, and dependent children are no longer generally eligible under that measure.
- The current IRCC family-member OWP pages still preserve the earlier exception that some in-Canada family members can extend a shortened already-issued work permit to align with the principal applicant's status when eligible.
- The bridging open work permit route still exists for eligible PR applicants, including Express Entry applicants and eligible provincial nominees. No new restriction affecting normal BOWP planning was found.
- The TR to PR pathway open work permit page still says applications can be made until December 31, 2026. No newer extension beyond that date was found.
- The Manitoba or Yukon support-letter open work permit public policy still appears active, allows permits of up to 2 years, and the IRCC page still shows it as open until December 31, 2025.
- IRCC's current category-based selection page still lists these categories: French-language proficiency, healthcare and social services, STEM, trades, education, transport, physicians with Canadian work experience, senior managers with Canadian work experience, researchers with Canadian work experience, and skilled military recruits.
- No new broad federal PR pathway was announced for ordinary OWP holders in this check window.
